'''''Cantharellus cinnabarinus''''', the red chanterelle, is a species of [[fungus]]. It is a member of the genus ''[[Cantharellus]]'' along with other [[chanterelle]]s. Its distinctive red color is imparted by the [[carotenoid]] canthaxanthin. Widely distributed in eastern Northern America, it fruits in association with [[hardwood]] trees in the summer and fall.

==Etymology==
It is named after [[cinnabar]], which has a similar red color.{{Citation needed|date=March 2024}}

== Description ==
''Cantharellus cinnabarinus'' is recognized by its distinctive flamingo-pink to bright orange and red colors (imparted by the carotenoid [[canthaxanthin]])<ref name=haxo50/> and the presence of false gills underneath the cap.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cantharellus cinnabarinus |url=https://www.mushroomexpert.com/cantharellus_cinnabarinus.html|access-date=2021-04-04|website=MushroomExpert.Com}}</ref> The fruitbodies can grow up to {{Convert|7|cm|frac=4}} across.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/664/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, California |pages=664 |orig-date=1979}}</ref>

It resembles some other species of ''[[Cantharellus]]'', but tends to be more small and slender.<ref name=":02">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=95}}</ref> Its forked gills distinguish it from ''[[Hygrocybe]]''.<ref name="Arora1986" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
Widely distributed in eastern Northern America,<ref name="mexp" /> it fruits in association with [[hardwood]] trees.<ref name="mill06" /> It can be found from June to October, mostly on the ground in broadleaf and mixed [[Broad-leaved tree|broadleaf]]/[[conifer]] forests. It usually occurs scattered or in small groups.<ref name=":02" /> It forms [[mycorrhiza]]l associations with forest trees and shows preference for [[acidic soil]]s.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Chanterelle – Identification, Distribution, Edibility, Ecology, Sustainable Harvesting |url=https://gallowaywildfoods.com/chanterelle-identification-distribution-edibility/|access-date=2021-04-04|website=Galloway Wild Foods}}</ref>

==Uses==
It is considered [[edible mushroom|edible]] and good.<ref name="Arora1986" />
