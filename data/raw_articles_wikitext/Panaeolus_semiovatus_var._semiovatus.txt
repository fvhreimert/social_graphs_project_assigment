'''''Panaeolus semiovatus'' var. ''semiovatus''''', also known as '''''P.&nbsp;semiovatus''''' and '''''Anellaria separata''''', and commonly known as the '''ringed panaeolus''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/355/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=355 |orig-date=1979}}</ref>{{efn|Other common names include '''shiny mottlegill''', '''common fungus of the feces variety''', and '''egghead mottlegill'''.{{citation needed|date=June 2025}}}} is a medium-sized buff-colored [[mushroom]] with black spores that [[Coprophilous fungi|grows on dung]].

==Description==
The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is up to {{Convert|9|cm|frac=4}} across, light tan then buff to whitish. It is oval then conical or parabolic.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is sticky when wet and often wrinkles when dry.  The [[Stipe (mycology)|stem]] is up to {{Convert|18|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} long and 12&nbsp;mm thick,<ref name="Arora1986" /> solid and smooth, with an [[Annulus (mycology)|annulus]] (ring) that is white, but often blackened by falling spores. The [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are [[wikt:adnexed|adnexed]], being wider in the middle, and narrowing at both ends; they are brown to black. The flesh is white, or straw-colored.<ref name=Phillips06>{{Cite book | author = Roger Phillips | year = 2006  | title = Mushrooms | publisher = Pan MacMillan | isbn = 0-330-44237-6}}</ref><ref name=T.Laessoe>{{Cite book | author = Thomas Laessoe | year = 1998 | title = Mushrooms (flexi bound) | publisher = Dorling Kindersley | isbn = 0-7513-1070-0}}</ref> The [[spore print]] is black.<ref name="Arora1986" />

==Habitat and distribution==
The species grows on horse dung.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is widely distributed and is present in many [[temperate zones]] of the world.

==Edibility==
Though nonpoisonous,<ref name="Miller 2006">{{cite book |last1=Miller Jr. |first1=Orson K. |author-link=Orson K. Miller Jr. |title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi |last2=Miller |first2=Hope H. |publisher=[[FalconGuide]] |year=2006 |isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1 |location=Guilford, CN |pages=234}}</ref> it is generally regarded as inedible and possessing a rather abysmal taste.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |publisher=Firefly Books |year=2010 |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |location=Buffalo, NY |page=236}}</ref> It has been unreliably rumored to contain the [[indole alkaloid]] [[psilocybin]].<ref name="Arora1986" /> Some people experience [[gastric upset]] after consumption.{{Citation needed|date=June 2025}}
