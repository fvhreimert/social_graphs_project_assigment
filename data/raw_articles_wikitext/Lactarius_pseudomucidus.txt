'''''Lactarius pseudomucidus''','' commonly known as the '''slimy milk cap''',<ref name=Arora1986>{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/77/mode/2up |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |location=Berkeley, California |year=1986 |orig-date=1979 |edition=2nd |pages=77}}</ref> is a [[mucilaginous]] species of [[fungus]] native to North America.

== Description ==
It has a charcoal brown [[Cap (mycology)|cap]], smooth and slimy, from {{Convert|2–10|cm|frac=4}} across, initially flat [[Convex shape|convex]], becoming shallowly depressed. The [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are [[decurrent]], white with a gray or yellow tinge, staining brownish.<ref name=":0">{{cite book |last1=Trudell |first1=Steve |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC |title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Ammirati |first2=Joe |publisher=Timber Press |year=2009 |isbn=978-0-88192-935-5 |series=Timber Press Field Guides |location=Portland, OR |pages=59–60}}</ref> The [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is {{Convert|2.5–10|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} tall and {{Convert|5-12|mm|frac=8}} thick;<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=179}}</ref> it is hollow and brittle. Both the cap and stipe are [[mucilaginous]].<ref name=":0" /> The flesh is gray and the [[latex]] is milky white, drying yellowish.<ref name=":0" /> There is only a slight odor, and the taste slowly becomes acrid. [[Basidiospore|Spores]] are white in mass, [[ellipsoid]], [[amyloid]], about 8&nbsp;[[μm]] long, with a [[wikt:reticulate|reticulate]] decoration on the surface.<ref>Hesler, LR, & AH Smith. 1979. North American species of Lactarius. Ann Arbor: University of Michigan Press.</ref><ref>Smith, AH. 1975. Field guide to western mushrooms. Ann Arbor: University of Michigan Press.</ref> The [[spore print]] is cream.<ref name="audu" />

The species is inedible,<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |orig-date=2005 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/118 118]}}</ref> with the extremely viscid cap and stalk being deterrent.<ref name=Arora1986/>

=== Similar species ===
It resembles ''[[Lactarius argillaceifolius]]'', which has a light orange-gray cap,<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=96–97|oclc=797915861}}</ref> and eastern North America's ''L.&nbsp;mucidus''.<ref name=":0" /> Additionally, ''[[Lactarius fumosus|L.&nbsp;fumosus]]'' and ''[[Lactifluus gerardi]]'' bear similarities.<ref name="audu" /> Other ''Lactarius'' species with slippery, grayish caps are either zonate and/or the gills stain purple.<ref name="audu" />

==Distribution and habitat==
It is native to northwestern North America, often found in coastal and [[conifer]] forests.<ref name=":0" />
