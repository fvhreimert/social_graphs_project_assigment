'''''Mycena crocata''''' is a species of [[agaric]] fungus in the family [[Mycenaceae]].<ref name="urlMycoBank:Mycena crocata">{{cite web |url=http://www.mycobank.org/MycoTaxo.aspx?Link=T&Rec=169501 |title=''Mycena crocata'' (Schrad.) P. Kumm. 1871   |publisher=[[MycoBank]]. International Mycological Association |access-date=2010-10-31}}</ref> The [[common name]] '''saffrondrop bonnet''' refers to the red or orange [[latex]] that it exudes if the [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is broken.<ref name="Collins">{{cite book|title=Complete British Mushrooms and Toadstools|year=2009|publisher=Collins|isbn=978-0-00-723224-6|author=Paul Sterry, Barry Hughes}}</ref> Its habitat is woody debris and [[leaf litter]] in [[deciduous]] woodland, especially [[beech]].<ref name="Collins"/>
