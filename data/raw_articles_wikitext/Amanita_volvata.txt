'''''Amanita volvata''''', also known as '''volvate amanita'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mushroomhunter.net/amanita_volvata2.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210117033828/http://mushroomhunter.net/amanita_volvata2.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 17, 2021|title=Volvate Amanita|date=July 6, 2007|accessdate=December 25, 2012}}</ref><ref name=NGSWG>{{NGSWG}}</ref> is an inedible<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=31}}</ref> white-coloured species of [[fungus|fungi]] from the family [[Amanitaceae]] found in the [[southeastern United States]]. Can be confused with ''[[Amanita ponderosa]]'', but that species is from the [[Iberian Peninsula|Iberian peninsula]]. The species is [[amyloid]] and have saccate [[volva (mycology)|volva]],<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zjvXkLpqsEgC&q=amanita+volvata&pg=PA29|author=Orson K. Miller, Hope Miller|title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi|publisher=A Falcon Guide|year=2006|page=29|isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1}}{{Dead link|date=August 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> and elliptic spores.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fungikingdom.net/mycology-education/amanita/amanita-043-001.html|title=Amanita volvata|accessdate=December 25, 2012}}</ref>
