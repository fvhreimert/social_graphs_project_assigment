'''''Rubroboletus lupinus''''', commonly known as the '''wolf bolete''', is a [[bolete]] fungus of the genus ''[[Rubroboletus]]''. Originally [[species description|described]] by [[Elias Magnus Fries]] in 1838 as species of ''[[Boletus]]'', it was transferred to ''[[Rubroboletus]]'' in 2015,<ref name="Vizzini 2015"/> a genus [[circumscription (taxonomy)|circumscribed]] to host other allied reddish-colored, blue-staining bolete species forming a distinct clade.<ref name="Zhao 2014"/> The species epithet is derived from the [[Latin]] word ''lupus'', meaning "wolf".

Molecular studies have revealed considerable genetic variation among European populations of ''R. lupinus'', placing it in a clade sister to ''[[Rubroboletus dupainii]]''.<ref name="Loizides 2019"/> The species is found in warm [[broad-leaved]] forests, forming [[ectomycorrhizal]] associations with various species of oak (''[[Quercus]]'') and sweet chestnut (''[[Castanea (plant)|Castanea]]'').
