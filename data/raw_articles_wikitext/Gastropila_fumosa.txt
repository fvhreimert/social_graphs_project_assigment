'''''Gastropila fumosa''''' is a species of [[puffball]] in the family [[Agaricaceae]]. It was first [[species description|described]] as ''Calvatia fumosa'' by American mycologist [[Sanford Myron Zeller]] in 1947,<ref name="Zeller 1947"/> and later transferred to ''[[Gastropila]]'' in 1976.<ref name="Ponce de León 1976"/> Some authors place it instead in the genus ''[[Handkea]]'', [[circumscription (taxonomy)|circumscribed]] by [[Hanns Kreisel]] in 1989.<ref name="Kreisel 1989"/>

==Description==
The [[basidiocarp|fruit body]] is anywhere from golf ball size to baseball size, round to oval, {{convert|3–8|cm|abbr=on|frac=8}} broad, thick, at first smooth and white, soon becoming grayish to brownish.<ref name="Arora 1986" /> The [[spores]] are firm and white at first, then yellowish or olive, and then dark brown and powdery.<ref name="Arora 1986" /> The species has an unpleasant smell while developing.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=268-269|language=en}}</ref> Its [[edible mushroom|edibility]] is unknown.<ref name="Arora 1986"/>

==Distribution and habitat==
The species fruits singly, in groups, or in small clusters on soil in [[spruce]]-[[fir]] forests in the [[Rocky Mountains]] and westward in the summer and fall.<ref name="McKnight 1987"/>
