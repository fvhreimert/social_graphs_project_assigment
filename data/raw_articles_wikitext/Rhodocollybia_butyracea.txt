'''''Rhodocollybia butyracea''''', commonly known as the '''buttery collybia''',<ref>[http://mushroomhobby.com/Gallery/Collybia/Rhodocollybia%20butyracea/ Rhodocollybia butyracea<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://bornnaturalist.org/images/view.cgi?src=edgewood/20090926-272;alt=buttery%20collybia%3Cbr%3E%3Ci%3ERhodocollybia%20butyracea%3C/i%3E;nofit=1 Local: image viewer<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Omphalotaceae]]. It has a number of [[subspecies]].<ref name=":1">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=119–120}}</ref>

==Description==
The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{Convert|2 to 10|cm|frac=4}} across. It is convex and becomes broadly convex or almost flat. When fresh, this species is smooth and moist. It has a reddish-brown colour fading to cinnamon.<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.mushroomexpert.com/rhodocollybia_butyracea.html Rhodocollybia butyracea (MushroomExpert.Com)<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

The gills are either free from the stem, or narrowly attached. They range from close to crowded and are whitish.<ref name=":1" /> Occasionally, they develop a pinkish tone as they age, and often form fine, jagged edges.

The stem is up to 10&nbsp;cm long and 1&nbsp;cm thick. It is normally somewhat club-shaped.<ref name=":1" /> It can either be moist or dry.

The flesh of this species is white. There is no distinctive odor or taste.

The spores are pale yellowish, pale pinkish, or white.<ref name=autogenerated1 />

=== Similar species ===
It can resemble ''[[Rhodocollybia badiialba|R.&nbsp;badiialba]]'', ''[[Rhodocollybia prolixa|R.&nbsp;prolixa]]'',<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=478}}</ref> and ''[[Gymnopus dryophilus]]''.<ref name=":1" />

==Habitat==
''Rhodocollybia butyracea'' is widely distributed in North America.<ref name=autogenerated1 />

== Ecology ==
This species is [[saprobic]]. It decomposes litter from conifers, usually that of the genus ''Pinus'',<ref name="autogenerated1" /> and occasionally hardwoods.<ref>[http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Rhodocollybia_butyracea.html California Fungi: Rhodocollybia butyracea<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

== Edibility ==
This mushroom is [[Edible mushroom|edible]], but unsubstantial,<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=70}}</ref> and can resemble inedible species.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/216/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=216 |orig-date=1979}}</ref>
