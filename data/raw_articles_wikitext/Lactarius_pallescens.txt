'''''Lactarius pallescens'''''  is a Western North American "milk-cap" mushroom, of which the milk turns violet when the flesh is damaged.  The fungi generally identified as ''L. pallescens'' are part of a [[species complex|complex]] of closely related species and varieties which have a peppery taste and are difficult to delimit definitively.<ref name="Mykoweb">Wood, Miichael (2013 January). ''Lactarius pallescens''.  On the ''MykoWeb.com'' Web site: [http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Lactarius_pallescens.html]
</ref>

The gray-brown cap ranges from 3 to 10 cm in width, with a mucilaginous surface,<ref name=":1">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=58}}</ref> whitish flesh and white [[latex]]. The [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are whitish and sometimes slightly decurrent.<ref name=":1" /> The viscid [[Stipe (mycology)|stalk]] ranges from 3 to 8 cm long and 1 to 2 cm wide. The spores are pale yellow to orange, elliptical, and bumpy. The flesh of the mushroom stains lilac.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=95|oclc=797915861}}</ref> In age, reddish stains develop.<ref name=":1" />

==Distribution==
''Lactarius pallescens'' is found on the [[West Coast of the United States]]. In the [[Pacific Northwest]], it can be found in [[conifer]] forests.<ref name=":1" />

==Related species==
''[[Lactarius uvidus]]'' (a close relative)<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> and ''Lactarius californiensis'' are similar.
