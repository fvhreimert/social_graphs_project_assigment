'''''Psathyrella piluliformis''''', commonly known as the '''clustered brittlestem''',<ref>{{Cite book |last=Siegel |first=Noah |title=Mushrooms of Cascadia: A Comprehensive Guide to Fungi of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |date=September 1, 2024 |publisher=Backcountry Press |isbn=9781941624197 |location=Humboldt County, CA |pages=101}}</ref> is a species of [[agaric]] fungus in the family [[Psathyrellaceae]].

== Description ==
It produces [[basidiocarp|fruit bodies]] ([[mushroom]]s) with broadly convex [[pileus (mycology)|caps]] measuring {{convert|2|–|5|cm|in|1|abbr=in|frac=8}} in diameter.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=235–236|oclc=797915861}}</ref> The caps are [[chestnut (color)|chestnut]] to reddish brown, the color fading with age and with dry weather.<ref name=":0" /> Fragments of the [[partial veil]] may remain on the cap margin, and as a wispy band of hairs on the stipe.<ref name=":0" />

The closely spaced [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] have an [[adnation|adnate]] attachment to the [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]].<ref name=":0" /> They are initially tan until the [[basidiospore|spores]] mature, when the gills turn dark brown.<ref name=":0" /> The stipe is 2–7&nbsp;cm tall and 3–7&nbsp;mm wide, white, smooth, hollow, and bulging at the base.<ref name=":0" /> The [[spore print]] is dark brown, sometimes purplish.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=609}}</ref>

=== Similar species ===
Similar species include ''Psathyrella carbonicola'', ''[[Psathyrella longipes|P.&nbsp;longipes]]'', ''[[Psathyrella longistriata|P.&nbsp;longistriata]]'', ''P.&nbsp;multipedata'', ''[[Psathyrella spadicea|P.&nbsp;spadicea]]'', and ''[[Parasola conopilus]]''.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />

== Habitat and distribution ==
Fruiting occurs in clusters at the base of [[hardwood]] stumps.<ref name=":0" />

== Uses ==
The species is considered edible but of low quality,<ref name="firstnature">{{Cite web|title=Psathyrella piluliformis, Common Stump Brittlestem mushroom|url=https://www.first-nature.com/fungi/psathyrella-piluliformis.php|access-date=2021-03-04|website=First-Nature.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=232}}</ref> with fragile flesh and being difficult to identify.<ref name=":1">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=203–204}}</ref>
