'''''Bjerkandera fumosa''''', the '''big smoky bracket''',<ref name="jarvis-2020">{{cite book |last1=Jarvis |first1=Peter J. |title=The Pelagic Dictionary of Natural History of the British Isles |date=2020 |publisher=Pelagic |location=Exeter |isbn=9781784271947}}</ref> is a species of [[polypore|poroid]] fungus in the family [[Phanerochaetaceae]].

==Taxonomy==
It was first [[species description|described]] to science as ''Boletus fumosus'' by [[Christiaan Hendrik Persoon]] in 1801.<ref name="Persoon 1801"/> [[Petter Adolf Karsten]] transferred the species to the genus ''[[Bjerkandera]]'' in 1879.<ref name="Karsten 1879"/>

==Description==
The form of ''Bjerkandera fumosa'' [[basidiocarp|fruit bodies]] ranges from effused-reflexed (spread out over the substrate and turned back at the margin to form a [[pileus (mycology)|cap]]) or cap-like, but attached directly to the substrate without a [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]]. These caps can be solitary or closely overlapping, and are often fused with neighbouring caps. The caps typically measure {{convert|5|–|10|cm|in|abbr=on}} wide by {{convert|2|cm|in|abbr=on|1}} wide, and a [[buff (colour)|buff]]-coloured upper surface with a texture ranging from finely hairy (tomentose) to smooth. The pores on the cap underside are circular to angular, numbering 2–5 per millimetre.<ref name="Ryvarden 2014"/>

''Bjerkandera fumosa'' has a monomitic [[hypha]]l system, containing only generative hyphae. The [[basidia]] are club-shaped, measuring 20–22&nbsp;[[micrometre|μm]]. [[Basidiospore|Spores]] have the shape of short cylinders, and measure 5.5–7 by 2.5–3.5&nbsp;μm. They are smooth, [[hyaline]], and do not react with [[Melzer's reagent]].<ref name="Ryvarden 2014"/>

==Habitat and distribution==
''Bjerkandera fumosa'' causes a [[Wood-decay fungus#White rot|white rot]] in various [[hardwood]] species. It has a [[Circumboreal Region|circumboreal]] distribution in the [[Northern Hemisphere]].<ref name="Ryvarden 2014"/>
