'''''Agaricus cupreobrunneus''''', commonly known as the copper mushrooom<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.britmycolsoc.org.uk/field_mycology/english-names |title=Archived copy |access-date=2024-01-25 |archive-date=2022-11-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221103000333/https://www.britmycolsoc.org.uk/field_mycology/english-names |url-status=dead }}</ref> or '''brown field mushroom''', is an [[edible mushroom]] of the genus ''[[Agaricus]]''.

== Description ==
The brown cap is {{Convert|3-10|cm|abbr=off|frac=2}} wide with flattened reddish-brown fibrils.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=225–226|oclc=797915861}}</ref> The white stalk is {{Convert|2-7|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}} tall and 1–2 cm wide.<ref name=":0" /> The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are dark brown, elliptical, and smooth.<ref name=":0" />

=== Similar species ===
''A. cupreobrunneus'' is similar in general appearance to a number of other ''Agaricus'' species, especially to ''A.&nbsp;campestris''.<ref name="MW" />  It also bears strong similarities to ''[[Agaricus argenteus|A.&nbsp;argenteus]]'', ''[[Agaricus augustus|A.&nbsp;augustus]]'', ''[[Agaricus hondensis|A.&nbsp;hondensis]]'', ''[[Agaricus porphyrocephalus|A.&nbsp;porphyrocephalus]]'', and ''[[Agaricus rutilescens|A.&nbsp;rutilescens]]''.<ref name=":0" /><ref name="Arora1986" /> The only potential lookalikes of ''A.&nbsp;cupreobrunneus'' that are [[poisonous mushroom|poisonous]] are yellow- or red-staining, or occur in much different habitats.<ref name="MW" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
''Agaricus cupreobrunneus'' tends to fruit in disturbed areas and grassy places, such as lawns, pastures, and roadsides.<ref name="MW"/> It can fruit by itself, gregariously, or in [[fairy ring]]s.<ref name=Arora1986/>

==Edibility==
''A. cupreobrunneus'' is [[edible mushroom|edible]] and good. Its taste is comparable to that of ''[[Agaricus campestris|A.&nbsp;campestris]]'', but it is comparatively lacking in texture.<ref name=Arora1986/> ''A.&nbsp;cupreobrunneus'' is not currently cultivated on a widespread basis, but is commonly eaten by collectors in the areas in which it grows.

It does not contain the carcinogen [[agaritine]], which appears in many other members of the genus ''[[Agaricus]]''.<ref name=Stijve/>
