'''''Astraeus pteridis''''', commonly known as the '''giant hygroscopic earthstar''',<ref name=Arora1986>{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/706/mode/2up |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |location=Berkeley, California |year=1986 |orig-date=1979 |edition=2nd |pages=706}}</ref> is a species of [[Astraeus (fungus)|false earthstar]] in the family [[Diplocystaceae]]. It is found in North America.

== Taxonomy ==
The species was described by American mycologist [[Cornelius Lott Shear]] in 1902 under the name ''Scleroderma pteridis''.<ref name="Shear1902" /> [[Sanford Myron Zeller]] transferred it to ''[[Astraeus (fungus)|Astraeus]]'' in 1948.<ref name="Zeller1948" />

== Description ==
''A. pteridis'' reaches {{Convert|5 to 15|cm|frac=4}} or more when expanded, and often has a more pronounced [[areolate]] pattern on the inner surface of the rays.<ref name=Arora1986/> Like other [[Astraeus (fungus)|''Astraeus'']] species, it is [[hygroscopic]], with rays expanding in humid conditions and closing in arid conditions. It is not typically considered edible.<ref name=Arora1986/>

It closely resembles the European [[Astraeus hygrometricus|''A.&nbsp;hygrometricus'']], but is larger.<ref name=Arora1986/> Within ''Astraeus'', ''A.&nbsp;pteridis'' is most closely related to ''[[Astraeus morganii|A.&nbsp;morganii]].''

== Distribution ==
A molecular [[phylogenetic]] study from 2013 resulted in the application of the name ''A.&nbsp;pteridis'' to the larger ''Astraeus'' found in the [[Pacific Northwest]] region of North America. ''A.&nbsp;pteridis'' has also been found in the [[Canary Islands]], [[Madeira]], and [[Argentina]], which share historical connections to [[Lusitania]]. It may be widely distributed or have been [[Species translocation|translocated]].<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Phosri|first1=Cherdchai|last2=Martín|first2=María P.|last3=Watling|first3=Roy|date=Dec 2013|title=Astraeus: hidden dimensions|journal=IMA Fungus|volume=4|issue=2|pages=347–356|doi=10.5598/imafungus.2013.04.02.13|issn=2210-6340|pmc=3905946|pmid=24563840}}</ref>
