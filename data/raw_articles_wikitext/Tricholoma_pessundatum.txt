'''''Tricholoma pessundatum''''' is a [[mushroom]] of the [[agaric]] genus ''[[Tricholoma]]''.

== Taxonomy ==
First described as ''Agaricus pessundatus'' by [[Elias Magnus Fries]] in 1821, it was transferred to the genus ''[[Tricholoma]]'' by [[Lucien Quélet]] in 1872.<ref name="Quélet 1872" />

== Description ==
The orangish cap is up to {{Convert|18|cm|frac=4}} across, with a lighter margin, and is viscid when wet. The [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are white but develop stains and vary in attachment. The stem is up to {{Convert|14|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} long. The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/185/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=185 |orig-date=1979}}</ref>

It has a sour meal odor.<ref name="Miller 2006">{{cite book |last=Miller Jr. |first=Orson K. |title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi |last2=Miller |first2=Hope H. |publisher=[[FalconGuide]] |year=2006 |isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1 |location=Guilford, CN |pages=127}}</ref>

=== Similar species ===
A very similar species to the European mushroom is ''[[Tricholoma muricatum]]'', which differs only in microscopic details.<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=105}}</ref>

== Toxicity ==
It contains toxins which can cause severe [[gastrointestinal upset]].<ref name="Miller 2006" />
