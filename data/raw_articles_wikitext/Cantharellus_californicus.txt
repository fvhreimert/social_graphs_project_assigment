'''''Cantharellus californicus''''', also called the '''California golden chanterelle''',<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |title=California AB261 {{!}} 2023-2024 {{!}} Regular Session |url=https://legiscan.com/CA/text/AB261/id/2653093 |access-date=2023-12-11 |website=LegiScan |language=en}}</ref> '''mud puppy''', or '''oak chanterelle''', is a [[fungus]] native to [[California]], United States.<ref name=kuo09/> It is a member of the genus ''[[Cantharellus]]'' along with other popular edible [[chanterelle]]s. It is generally similar in appearance to ''[[Cantharellus cibarius|C.&nbsp;cibarius]]'' and ''[[Cantharellus formosus|C.&nbsp;formosus]]'' except for its large size at maturity.

==Description==


The [[pileus (mycology)|pileus]] (cap) of ''C.&nbsp;californicus'' is {{convert|5|–|30|cm|in|round=0.5}} wide,<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=270–272|oclc=797915861}}</ref> exceptionally {{convert|50|cm|in|abbr=on}}, and yellow-orange in color (although adhering leaf litter may cause a mottled color); it may become brownish with age. The [[hymenium]] is folded into [[decurrent]] ridges (false gills) and cross-veins, which deepen with age. The color of these ridges is usually similar to the cap but paler. The [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] (stem) is {{convert|2-10|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} long and {{convert|1-4|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} wide,<ref name=":0" /> with coloration similar to the hymenium.<ref name=AroraDunham/> The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are creamy yellow, elliptical, and smooth.<ref name=":0" />

individual specimens up to {{convert|2|kg|lb}} are reported, making it the largest-known species of chanterelle.<ref name="AroraDunham" />  Their unusual size is due in part to their capacity for [[indeterminate growth]], making ''C.&nbsp;californicus'' specimens actively grow for far longer than most other mushrooms.<ref name="BAMS" />

=== Similar species ===
Several other species of chanterelle may be found in western North America:
*''[[Cantharellus cascadensis|C. cascadensis]]''<ref name=":0" /> – bright yellow fading to white in center of cap, associated with conifers
*''[[Cantharellus cibarius|C. cibarius]]'' var. ''roseocanus''<ref name=":0" /> – false gills tend to be as dark or darker than cap
*''[[Cantharellus formosus|C. formosus]]''<ref name=":0" /> – smaller size, narrower stem, associated with conifers
*''[[Cantharellus subalbidus|C. subalbidus]]'' – whitish overall color

Additionally, ''[[Hygrophoropsis aurantiaca]]'', ''[[Chroogomphus|Chroogomphus tomentosus]]'', and species in the genera ''[[Craterellus]]'', ''[[Gomphus (fungus)|Gomphus]]'', ''[[Omphalotus]]'', and ''[[Polyozellus]]'' may have a somewhat similar appearance to ''C.&nbsp;californicus''. ''[[Omphalotus olivascens]]'', the western jack-o'-lantern mushroom, is poisonous and has been mistaken for chanterelles.

==Distribution and habitat==
''Cantharellus californicus'' forms a [[mycorrhizal]] association with [[Quercus|oak]]s, particularly [[Quercus agrifolia|coast live oak]] in the [[California oak woodland|woodland]]s of [[Coastal California]]. It has also been found in association with [[Quercus wislizeni|interior live oak]], [[Quercus kelloggii|California black oak]], [[Quercus chrysolepis|canyon live oak]], [[Lithocarpus densiflorus|tanoak]], and possibly [[Arbutus menziesii|Pacific madrone]] and [[Arctostaphylos|manzanita]].<ref name=AroraDunham/> ''C.&nbsp;californicus'' is a popular wild edible in the [[San Francisco Bay Area]], and is most common between November and April.<ref name=AroraDunham/><ref name=perry08/>

==In culture==
It became the official state mushroom of California in 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-12-23 |title=Meet the Golden Chanterelle, CA's new official state mushroom starting 2024 |url=https://abc7news.com/california-state-mushroom-golden-chanterelle-cantharellus-californicus-largest-in-the-world/14219460/ |access-date=2024-03-09 |website=ABC7 San Francisco |language=en}}</ref>
