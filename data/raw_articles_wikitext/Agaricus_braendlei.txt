'''''Agaricus braendlei''''' is a species of mushroom in the family [[Agaricaceae]]. It is found in [[North America]], and closely related to ''[[Agaricus campestris|A.&nbsp;campestris]]''.<ref>L.A. Parra & M.M. G贸mez (2019). Micologia e Vegetazione Mediterranea 33 (2): 75</ref> Both species are popular edibles characterized by white, slightly furry caps, bright pink gills which turn to chocolate brown as the spore mature, and no staining reaction when bruised or scratched.{{cn|date=April 2025}} Like most ''Agaricus'' species, this species has a ring on the stalk and no volva.{{cn|date=April 2025}}

== Taxonomy ==
For a long time, it was thought that ''Agaricus campestris'' occurred in North America. However, Richard Kerrigan researched North American ''Agaricus'' species and found that ''A. campestris'' likely does not occur in North America. Instead, several different species, including ''A. braendlei'', occur in North America.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-03-14 |title=Meadow Mushroom: Identification, Foraging, and Cooking Guide - Mushroom Appreciation |url=https://www.mushroom-appreciation.com/meadow-mushroom.html |access-date=2025-08-19 |website=www.mushroom-appreciation.com |language=en-US}}</ref>
