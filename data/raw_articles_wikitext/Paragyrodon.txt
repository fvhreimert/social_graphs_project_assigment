'''''Paragyrodon''''' is a [[genus]] of [[fungi]] in the family [[Paxillaceae]].<ref name=Kirk2008/> It is a [[monotypic]] genus, containing the single species '''''Paragyrodon sphaerosporus'''''. ''Paragyrodon'' was circumscribed by [[Rolf Singer]] in 1942.<ref name=Singer1942/>

== Description ==
The caps are {{Convert|4-12|cm|frac=4}} wide and a blotchy combination of yellow to brown. The stem is {{Convert|3-7|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} tall and {{Convert|1.2-3|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} thick; it stains brown.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=316}}</ref>

== Distribution and habitat ==
The species is distributed mostly around the [[Great Lakes Region]], however, the species has also been observed further west in [[Kansas]], [[Iowa]] and [[Colorado]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kuo|first1=M|title=Paragyrodon sphaerosporus|url=http://www.mushroomexpert.com/paragyrodon_sphaerosporus.html|website=MushroomExpert.Com|access-date=17 August 2014}}</ref>  The species has been associated with the [[white oak]] and the [[gambel oak]] in Colorado.
