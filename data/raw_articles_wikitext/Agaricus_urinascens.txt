'''''Agaricus urinascens''''' (the '''macro mushroom''')<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.britmycolsoc.org.uk/field_mycology/english-names |title=Archived copy |access-date=2024-01-25 |archive-date=2022-11-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221103000333/https://www.britmycolsoc.org.uk/field_mycology/english-names |url-status=dead }}</ref> is a species of fungus in the family [[Agaricaceae]].<ref>{{cite web|title=''Agaricus urinascens''|website=Mycobank|url= https://www.MycoBank.org/MB/292321}}</ref>

The mushroom grows up to {{Convert|30|cm}} wide and {{Convert|12|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} tall. The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is whitish, domed then becoming convex in age. The gills are whitish, then darken to pink or brownish with age. The flesh is white with an [[almond]] scent.<ref name="tfb">{{Cite book |last=Francis-Baker |first=Tiffany |title=Concise Foraging Guide |date=2021 |publisher=[[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] |isbn=978-1-4729-8474-6 |series=[[The Wildlife Trusts]] |location=London |pages=158}}</ref>

It can be found in pastures of Europe from June to September.<ref>{{cite book |title=Concise Mushroom Guide |publisher=Bloomsbury Wildlife |year=2019 |ISBN=978-1472963789 |series=The Wildlife Trusts |page=122}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Buczacki |first1=S. |title=Collins Fungi Guide |last2=Shields |first2=C. |last3=Ovenden |first3=D. |publisher=HarperCollins |year=2012 |ISBN=978-0007242900 |page=46}}</ref> It is [[Edible mushroom|edible]] when cooked and can be dried for preservation.<ref name="tfb" />
