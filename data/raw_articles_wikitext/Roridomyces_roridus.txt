'''''Roridomyces roridus''''', commonly known as the '''dripping bonnet''' or the '''slippery mycena''', is a species of [[agaric]] fungus in the family [[Mycenaceae]].

The mushroom [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is tannish, fading to whitish or dirty yellow in color, broadly convex, and {{convert|5|–|15|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} in diameter.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/237/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=237 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> The whitish [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is covered with a thick, slippery slime layer.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The species can be [[bioluminescent]], and is one of the several causative species of [[foxfire (bioluminescence)|foxfire]].<ref name="Desjardin 2008" />

It resembles some other members of the genus.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is of no known culinary value due to its small size and slippiriness.<ref name="Arora1986" />
