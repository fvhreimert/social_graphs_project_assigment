'''''Psilocybe atlantis''''' is a rare [[psychedelic mushroom]] that contains [[psilocybin]] and [[psilocin]] as main active compounds. It is a close relative of ''[[Psilocybe mexicana]]'' and has been recorded only from [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]].<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Guzmán | first1 = G. | last2 = Hanlin | first2 = R. T. | last3 = White | first3 = C. | date = 2003 | title = Another new bluing species of ''Psilocybe'' from Georgia, U.S.A. | journal = Mycotaxon | volume = 86 | pages = 179–183 }}</ref> It has a pleasant taste and smell.

While naturally rare it is often cultivated for its psychedelic properties.

==Description==
The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is 2.5–4&nbsp;cm in diameter, [[conic]] to [[wiktionary:convex|convex]], and smooth to slightly striate, sometimes with a small [[umbo (mycology)|umbo]]. The cap surface is pale brown to reddish brown in color, [[hygrophanous]], and bruises blue where damaged.<br>
Its gills are [[wikt:adnate|subadnate]], thin, and brown.<br>
The [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is 5&nbsp;cm by .3&nbsp;cm. It has an equal structure and is brownish with small brown scales, especially towards the base. The stipe also bruises blue where damaged.<br>
''Psilocybe atlantis'' [[spore]]s are 9 x 6 x 5.5 [[μm]] with a broad [[germ pore]].

==Distribution and habitat==
''Psilocybe atlantis'' has been found in grassy lawns and vacant lots in [[Fulton County, Georgia]].

The original find was made in Fulton County Georgia, growing in a patch of moss beside a 5 year old home whose lot bordered a wooded area within 50 meters of a tributary of the Chattahoochee river.
