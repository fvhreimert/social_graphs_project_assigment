'''''Flammulina velutipes''''', the '''velvet foot''', '''velvet stem''', '''velvet shank''' or '''wild enoki''',<ref name="BMS" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite book |last1=Sisson |first1=Liv |url=https://www.worldcat.org/title/on1372569849 |title=Fungi of Aotearoa: a curious forager's field guide |last2=Vigus |first2=Paula |date=2023 |publisher=Penguin Books |isbn=978-1-76104-787-9 |location=Auckland, New Zealand |pages=112 |oclc=on1372569849 }}</ref><ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/220/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=220 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> is a species of gilled mushroom in the family [[Physalacriaceae]]. The species occurs in Europe and North America. 

== Taxonomy ==
The species was originally described from England by botanist [[William Curtis]] in 1782 as ''Agaricus velutipes''. It was transferred to the genus ''[[Flammulina]]'' by [[Rolf Singer]] in 1951.

Until recently, ''F. velutipes'' was considered to be [[conspecific]] with the Asian'' [[Flammulina filiformis|F.&nbsp;filiformis]]'', cultivated for food as "enokitake" or "golden needle mushroom", but [[DNA sequencing]] shows that the two are distinct.<ref name="Wang" />

== Description ==
''F. velutipes'' grows up to {{Convert|10|cm|frac=4}} tall and wide.<ref name="tfb">{{Cite book |last=Francis-Baker |first=Tiffany |title=Concise Foraging Guide |date=2021 |publisher=[[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] |isbn=978-1-4729-8474-6 |series=[[The Wildlife Trusts]] |location=London |pages=137}}</ref> The cap is light orange, darker toward the center.<ref name="tfb" /> The stem fades to a darker color near the base.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=449}}</ref> The flesh of the cap is yellow, while that of the stem is brown. The odour and flavour are pleasant.<ref name="tfb" /> The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="Arora1986" />

=== Similar species ===
It may resemble ''[[Flammulina populicola|F. populicola]]'' and ''[[Flammulina lupinicola|F.&nbsp;lupinicola]]''. ''[[Marasmiellus dichrous]]'' and ''[[Xeromphalina tenuipes]]'' are also similar.<ref name="audu" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
The species occurs throughout Europe (October to February)<ref name="tfb" /> and in North America (through May in the east).<ref name="audu" />

Velvet shank is [[Saprotrophic nutrition|saprotrophic]] especially on dead or dying elm, as well as ash, beech and oak.<ref>{{Cite web |title=''Flammulina velutipes'', Velvet Shank mushroom |url=https://first-nature.com/fungi/flammulina-velutipes.php |access-date=May 12, 2024 |website=first-nature.com}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

==Uses==
The fleshy caps can be sliced and cooked (ideally with the skin removed),<ref name="Arora1986" /> or dried and ground into seasoning.<ref name="tfb" />
