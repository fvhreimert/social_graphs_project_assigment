'''''Russula subnigricans''''', known as the '''rank russula''',<ref name="Arora 1986">{{cite book |author=Arora D. |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsdemysti00aror_0/page/90 |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=Ten Speed Press |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-169-5 |location=Berkeley, California |pages=90-91 |url-access=registration}}</ref> or '''Nise-Kurohatsu''' ([[Japanese language|Japanese]]), meaning "false [[Russula nigricans|blackening russula]]" is a [[basidiomycete]] [[mushroom]] of the genus ''[[Russula]]'' found in East Asia. It is [[Mushroom poisoning|poisonous]].

==Taxonomy==
The species was named by Japanese mycologist [[Tsuguo Hongo]] in 1955.<ref name="Hongo(1955)" />

The name was formerly applied to the North American fungus ''Russula eccentrica'' in [[California]].<ref>{{cite web |year=2007 |title=California Fungi:''Russula eccentrica'' |url=http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Russula_eccentrica.html |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080510145729/http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Russula_eccentrica.html |archivedate=2008-05-10 |accessdate=2008-02-12 |work=The Fungi of California website |publisher=Mykoweb |vauthors=Wood M, Stevens F}}</ref> It has been reclassified as ''Russula cantharellicola'', where it grows in association with ''[[Quercus agrifolia]]'' (coast live oak) trees in [[California oak woodland]] habitats.<ref>[http://openjournals.wsu.edu/index.php/pnwfungi/article/view/1279 Openjournals.wsu.edu: "A new species of Russula, subgenus Compactae from California"] (2014).</ref>

==Description==
The flesh turns pale red when cut, but does not turn black, unlike ''[[Russula nigricans]]''.<ref name="Hongo(1955)" />

== Toxicity ==
''Russula subnigricans'' is a [[poisonous mushroom]],<ref name="Arora 1986" /> and has been responsible for mushroom poisoning in [[Taiwan]] and Japan. The effect is a serious one, [[rhabdomyolysis]].

The toxins responsible are the very unusual [[cycloprop-2-ene carboxylic acid]] (a&nbsp;toxic molecule consisting of only 10 atoms) and Russuphelin&nbsp;A (a&nbsp;heavily chlorinated [[polyphenolic]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/scienceNews/idUKTRE54N1HI20090524|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090530114445/http://uk.reuters.com/article/scienceNews/idUKTRE54N1HI20090524|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 30, 2009|title=Experts identify toxic compound in deadly mushroom |last=Editorial |website=Reuters.com|access-date=19 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|vauthors=Takahashi A, Agatsuma T, Matsuda M, Ohta T, Nunozawa T, Endo T, Nozoe S |year=1992|title=Russuphelin A, a new cytotoxic substance from the mushroom ''Russula subnigricans'' Hongo. |journal=Chem Pharm Bull  |volume=40 |issue=12 |pages=3185–88 |pmid=1294320 |doi=10.1248/cpb.40.3185|doi-access=free }}</ref>
