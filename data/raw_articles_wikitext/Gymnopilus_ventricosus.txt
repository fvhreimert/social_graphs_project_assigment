'''''Gymnopilus ventricosus''''', commonly known as the '''jumbo gym'''<ref name=":1">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |title=All that the Rain Promises and More: A Hip Pocket Guide to Western Mushrooms |date=February 1, 1991 |isbn=9780898153880 |pages=139 |language=en}}</ref> or '''western jumbo gym''',<ref name=":02">{{Cite book |last=Siegel |first=Noah |title=Mushrooms of Cascadia: A Comprehensive Guide to Fungi of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |date=September 1, 2024 |publisher=Backcountry Press |isbn=9781941624197 |location=Humboldt County, CA |pages=137}}</ref> is a species of [[mushroom]] in the family [[Hymenogastraceae]].  It was first [[Species description|described]] from the [[Stanford University]] campus by [[Franklin Sumner Earle]] as ''Pholiota ventricosa'' in 1902.

==Description==
The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{convert|5|to|30|cm|in|1|abbr=on|frac=8}} or larger in diameter.<ref name="MOTRC-2016">{{cite book |last1=Siegel |first1=Noah |title=Mushrooms of the Redwood Coast: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fungi of Coastal Northern California |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |publisher=Clarkson Potter/Ten Speed |year=2016 |isbn=9781607748182 |page=152 |lccn=2015027853 |oclc=956478776}}</ref><ref name=Hesler1969/><ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=182}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |first1=Jenny | last1 = Westerhoff | title=Big Laughing Gym (Laughing Jim): Comprehensive Identification Guide - Mushroom Appreciation |url=https://www.mushroom-appreciation.com/big-laughing-gym.html |website=www.mushroom-appreciation.com |access-date=3 January 2025 |date=12 September 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Stevens |first1=Michael Wood & Fred |title=California Fungi: Gymnopilus ventricosus |url=https://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Gymnopilus_ventricosus.html |website=www.mykoweb.com |access-date=3 January 2025}}</ref> The stalk is thick and bulbous, with a membranous [[Annulus (mycology)|ring]].<ref name=":0" /> This species contains no [[psilocybin]], and is considered inedible; it is very bitter.<ref name="MOTRC-2016"/><ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/198 198]}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

== Habitat and ecology ==
''Gymnopilus ventricosus'' grows on dead wood. It is found on both [[Conifer|conifers]] and [[Hardwood|hardwoods]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Stevens |first=Michael Wood & Fred |title=California Fungi: Gymnopilus ventricosus |url=http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Gymnopilus_ventricosus.html |access-date=2025-10-20 |website=www.mykoweb.com}}</ref>
