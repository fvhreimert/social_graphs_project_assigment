'''''Russula acrifolia''''' is a species of [[mushroom]]. Its [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is coloured grey to blackish-grey; the cap becomes red when it is injured, but then turns blackish-gray. It is [[edible mushroom|edible]] and described as having an acrid taste. It grows on rich soils.<ref name="mtsn"/>

==Distribution==
''Russula acrifolia'' is a [[holarctic]] species that needs a [[temperate climate]]. The species is spread in the [[Caucasus]], [[Siberia]], [[Korea]] and [[Japan]], [[Northern America]], [[Northern Africa]] and [[Europe]].<ref name="mtsn"/>

==Ecological properties==
''Russula acrifolia'' is a [[mycorrhiza]]l mushroom for different trees. Its favourite symbionic partners are ''[[Fagus sylvatica]]'' and [[spruces|spruce]]. If those are not available, it can also form symbiotic partnerships with [[larix]], [[pine]]s, [[betula]], [[oak]]s and [[tilia]].<ref name="mtsn"/>
