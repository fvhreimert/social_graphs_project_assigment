'''''Gymnopilus chrysimyces''''' is a species of [[mushroom]] in the family [[Hymenogastraceae]].

==Medicinal==
In a 1982 study, this species was shown to contain [[hemagglutinins]]. Proteins from ''G. chrysimyces'' showed activity towards rat [[erythrocytes]], while proteins from ''Lentinus squarrosulus'' showed activity towards guinea pig and mouse erythrocytes. The [[agglutination (biology)|agglutination]] of proteins from the two species showed that both have more than one hemagglutinin.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Hemagglutinating activity in extracts of mycelia from submerged mushroom cultures |journal=Applied and Environmental Microbiology |date=October 1982 |first=PC |last=Banjoree |author2=Ghosh, A.K. |author3=Sengupta, S.  |volume=44 |issue=4 |pages=1009â€“1011 |url=http://aem.asm.org/content/44/4/1009.full.pdf+html |format=PDF |access-date=2013-09-11}}</ref>
