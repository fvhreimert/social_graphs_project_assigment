'''''Candolleomyces candolleanus''''' (formerly known as ''Psathyrella candolleana''), commonly known as the '''common brittlestem''',<ref>{{Cite book |last=Siegel |first=Noah |title=Mushrooms of Cascadia: A Comprehensive Guide to Fungi of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |date=September 1, 2024 |publisher=Backcountry Press |isbn=9781941624197 |location=Humboldt County, CA |pages=101}}</ref> is a species of fungus in the family [[Psathyrellaceae]]. The color is tannish when young, fading to white. It is found in lawns in North America.

== Description ==
The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is tan when young,<ref name=":1">{{cite book |last1=Trudell |first1=Steve |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC |title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Ammirati |first2=Joe |publisher=Timber Press |year=2009 |isbn=978-0-88192-935-5 |series=Timber Press Field Guides |location=Portland, OR |pages=202}}</ref> fading to whitish,<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=592}}</ref> and growing to {{convert|2|–|8|cm|in|abbr=on|round=0.5}} in diameter;<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=233–234|oclc=797915861}}</ref> they are initially conical, later becoming rounded and finally with upturned margins in maturity. The cap margin is irregular and radially asymmetrical—a defining characteristic of this species. It can retain [[Veil (mycology)|veil]] fragments on the edge and center.<ref name=":1" /> The white stalk<ref name=":1" /> is {{Cvt|4-10|cm|round=0.5}} tall and 3–7&nbsp;mm wide.<ref name=":0" /> The [[spore print]] is purple-brown, while [[basidiospore|spore]]s are smooth and elliptical, measuring 6.5–8 by 4–5&nbsp;[[μm]].<ref name="Huffman 2008" />

=== Similar species ===
One similar species is ''[[Psathyrella gracilis]]''.<ref name=":0" /> Some species may have darker caps when young, drying to match that of ''C.&nbsp;candolleanus''.<ref name=":1" /> Also similar are ''[[Candolleomyces tuberculatus|C.&nbsp;tuberculatus]]'', ''[[Britzelmayria multipedata]]'', and members of ''[[Coprinopsis]]'' and ''[[Psathyrella]]''.<ref name="audu" />

== Etymology ==
The [[botanical name|specific epithet]] ''candolleanus'' honors Swiss botanist [[Augustin Pyramus de Candolle]].<ref name="Evenson 1997" />

== Distribution ==
It is commonly found growing in small groups around stumps and tree roots on [[lawn]]s and [[pasture]]s<ref name=":1" /> in North America<ref name="audu" /> and Europe.{{Citation needed|date=March 2025}} In 2014, it was reported from [[Iraq]].<ref name="Al-Habib 2014" />

== Edibility ==
While it is edible and may have a good flavor, it is not recommended due to its thin flesh, alleged poor culinary value and consistency, as well as difficulty in identification.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Jones |first=Julie |title=Psathyrella candolleana |url=http://biology.burke.washington.edu/herbarium/imagecollection/taxon.php?Taxon=Psathyrella%20candolleana |access-date=2022-08-01 |website=Burke Herbarium Image Collection}}</ref><ref name="Miller 2006">{{cite book|last1=Miller Jr.|first1=Orson K.|title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi|last2=Miller|first2=Hope H.|publisher=[[FalconGuide]]|year=2006|isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1|location=Guilford, CN|pages=238|author-link=Orson K. Miller Jr.}}</ref>
