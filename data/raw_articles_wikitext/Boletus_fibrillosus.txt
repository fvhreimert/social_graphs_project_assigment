'''''Boletus fibrillosus''''', commonly known as the '''fib king''',<ref>{{Cite web |last=Initiative |first=The Global Fungal Red List |title=Boletus fibrillosus |url=https://redlist.info/iucn/species_view/309681/ |access-date=2024-02-04 |website=redlist.info |language=en}}</ref> is a [[basidiomycete]] fungus of the genus ''[[Boletus]]''. The [[holotype]] was collected in [[Mendocino County, California]].<ref name=thiers75/><ref name=bess00/> [[Phylogenetic]] analysis has shown it to be a member of a [[clade]] (closely related group) with ''[[Boletus pinophilus|B.&nbsp;pinophilus]]'', ''[[Boletus regineus|B.&nbsp;regineus]]'', ''[[Boletus rex-veris|B.&nbsp;rex-veris]]'', ''[[Boletus subcaerulescens|B.&nbsp;subcaerulescens]]'', and ''[[Gastroboletus subalpinus]]''.<ref name="dent10" />

The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is up to {{Convert|17|cm|frac=2}} wide and buff to brown to dark brown, with a wrinkled to finely fibrous texture.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/523/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=523 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> The tubes are yellow, while the flesh is white to buff and does not stain when cut.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The [[stipe (mycology)|stem]] is up to 16&nbsp;cm long,<ref name="Arora1986" /> yellowish at the top, brown otherwise, with a reticulate texture,<ref name="thiers75" /><ref name="bess00" /> and [[mycelium]] enshrouding the bottom.<ref name=":0" />

The species has only been found in western North America.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The [[basidiocarp|fruiting bodies]] are found in mixed coastal forests in the fall, usually singly or in small groups.<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=217}}</ref> It is [[Edible mushroom|edible]], but considered to have inferior taste to other edible boletes such as ''[[Boletus edulis|B.&nbsp;edulis]]'', which it is often confused with.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Trudell|first=Steve|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|location=Portland, Oregon|pages=217}}</ref>
