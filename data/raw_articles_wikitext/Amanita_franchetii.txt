'''''Amanita franchetii''''', also known as '''Franchet's amanita''',<ref name=NGSWG>{{NGSWG}}</ref> is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Amanitaceae]].

==Taxonomy==
It was given its current name by Swiss mycologist [[Victor Fayod]] in 1889 in honor of French botanist [[Adrien René Franchet]].<ref name="Fayod1889" />

As ''A. aspera'' var. ''franchetii'', it had the name '''yellow-veiled amanita'''.<ref name="Arora1986" />

There exists a [[Variety (botany)|variety]] known as ''A.&nbsp;franchetii'' var. ''lactella'' that is entirely white except for the bright yellow universal veil remnants.<ref name="lactella" />

==Description==
The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{Convert|5–12|cm|abbr=off|frac=2}} wide, and is yellow-brown to brown in color. The flesh is white or pale yellow and has a mild odor.<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=68–69|oclc=797915861}}</ref> The closely spaced [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are the same color as the flesh. The [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is thick and larger at the base, also white to yellowish; loose areas of yellow veil form on the base. A thick ring is left by the [[partial veil]].<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=82–83}}</ref>

=== Similar species ===
A similar fungus in western [[North America]] was also referred to as ''A.&nbsp;franchetii'', but was long suspected of being a separate, undescribed species,<ref name="Tuloss11" /> and in 2013 was formally described under the name ''[[Amanita augusta|A.&nbsp;augusta]]''.<ref>Bojantchev D, Davis RM. (2013.) [http://www.pnwfungi.org/pdf_files/manuscripts_volume_8/naf20135.pdf ''Amanita augusta'', a new species from California and the Pacific Northwest.] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130502094440/http://www.pnwfungi.org/pdf_files/manuscripts_volume_8/naf20135.pdf|date=2013-05-02}} ''North American Fungi'' 8(5):1-11. {{doi|10.2509/naf2013.008.005}}</ref>

==Distribution and habitat==
''A. franchetii'' occurs in [[Europe]] and [[North Africa]] with oaks (''[[Quercus]]'' ssp.), chestnuts (''[[Castanea (genus)|Castanea]]'' ssp.), and pines (''[[Pinus]]'' ssp.).<ref name="Tuloss11" />

''A. franchetii'' var. ''lactella'' is found in the western [[Mediterranean region]], associated with several species of oak (''[[Quercus suber]]'' and ''[[Quercus robur|Q. robur]]'') and hornbeam (''[[Carpinus betulus]]''),<ref name="lactella" /> and is also reported from [[Serbia]].<ref name="Lukic" />

==Edibility==
''A. franchetii'' is considered inedible,<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=28}}</ref><ref name=Arora1986/> and is reported as being toxic when raw or undercooked.<ref name=":0" /> Although the species was implicated in the 2005 deaths of ten people in China who displayed symptoms similar to those caused by [[alpha-Amanitin]] poisoning,<ref name=bmj/> this case report has been called into question for possible misidentification of the mushrooms involved.<ref name="PubPeer">{{cite journal| journal=PubPeer| title=''Outbreak of fatal mushroom poisoning with Amanita franchetii and Ramaria rufescens''| date=22 February 2009| url=https://www.pubpeer.com/publications/4AAFDDE2F13305160A916F89031C79| access-date=2022-07-06| last1=Huang| first1=Liang| last2=Liu| first2=Xue Lan| last3=Cao| first3=Chun Shui| last4=Ying| first4=Qing| volume=2009| pages=bcr0620080327| doi=10.1136/bcr.06.2008.0327| pmid=21686856| pmc=3029993}}</ref>
