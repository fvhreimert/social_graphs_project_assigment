'''''Polyporus umbellatus''''' is an [[edible mushroom|edible]] species of mushroom. It is also called  '''umbrella polypore'''.

==Description==
The fruit body is composed of numerous (sometimes several hundred) caps. They are {{Convert|1–4|cm|frac=4}} across,<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=282}}</ref> deeply umbilicate, light brown, and form the extremities of a strong, many branched stalk. The compound fungus can be up to 40&nbsp;cm in diameter. The pores are decurrent, narrow and whitish.<ref name="audu" /> The stalk is whitish grey, {{Convert|2.5-7|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}},<ref name="audu" /> and originates from a strong, underground, tuber-like nodule. The flesh is white and soft,<ref name="audu" /> hardening with age. The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="audu" />

=== Similar species ===
The caps of ''[[Grifola frondosa]]'' are less consistently sized and those of ''[[Armillaria tabescens]]'' have gills.<ref name="audu" />

==Habitat and distribution==
The species grows on roots of old [[beech]]es or [[oak]].<ref>Kunca V., 2011: Ecology and incidence of Polyporus umbellatus in Slovakia. Czech Mycology 63(1): 39–53. [http://www.czechmycology.org/_cm/CM63104.pdf]</ref> It can be found in eastern North America from May to September.<ref name="audu" />

==Uses==
It is a choice [[edible mushroom]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-06-25 |title=Polyporus umbellatus |url=https://midwestmycology.org/polyporus-umbellatus/ |access-date=2022-11-09 |website=Midwest American Mycological Information |language=en-US}}</ref>
