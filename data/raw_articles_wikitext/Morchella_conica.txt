'''''Morchella conica''''' is an old [[binomial nomenclature|binomial name]] previously applied to species of [[fungi]] in the [[family (biology)|family]] [[Morchellaceae]]. It is one of three scientific names that had been commonly used to describe '''black morels''', the others being ''[[Morchella angusticeps|M.&nbsp;angusticeps]]'' and ''[[Morchella elata|M.&nbsp;elata]]''. It was first introduced by mycologist [[Christian Hendrik Persoon]] in 1818,<ref name=persoon/> as a superfluous name for the old taxon '''''Morchella continua'''''. According to Richard and colleagues, Fries’ sanctioning applies only at the subgeneric level and the name is [[Nomen illegitimum|illegitimate]].<ref name=Richard2014/>

Throughout the years, the name ''M. conica'' has been invariably applied to many different species by different authors, and [[Molecular phylogenetics|DNA analysis]] in 2014 revealed that morels identified as "''M. conica''" indeed belonged to ''[[Morchella deliciosa]]'', ''[[Morchella purpurascens]]'', ''[[Morchella tridentina]]'', and ''[[Morchella vulgaris]]''.<ref name=Richard2014/>
