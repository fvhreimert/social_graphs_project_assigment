'''''Hygrophorus marzuolus''''', commonly known as the '''March mushroom''', is a [[species]] of [[fungus]] in the family [[Hygrophoraceae]]. It is known from Asia, Europe, and North America, where it grows on the ground in [[mixed forest]]s at high elevations.

==Taxonomy==
The species was originally [[species description|described]] by [[Elias Magnus Fries]] in 1821 as ''Agaricus marzuolus'', and transferred to  ''[[Hygrophorus]]'' in 1893 by [[Giacomo Bresadola]].<ref name="urlMycoBank: Hygrophorus marzuolus"/> It is [[common name|commonly]] known as the "March mushroom".<ref name="McKnight 1987"/>

==Description==
The fruit bodies have [[pileus (mycology)|caps]] that are broadly convex, measuring {{convert|2.5|–|11|cm|in|1|abbr=on}} in diameter. Their surfaces are smooth and sticky, with a pale greyish-brown center and darker brown to blackish-grey margin. The distantly spaced, broad [[lamella (mycology)|gills]] have an [[adnation|adnate]] to adnexed attachment to the [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]], and two tiers of intervening lamellulae (short gills). The gills are initially white before turning gray to bluish-gray in age. The whitish stipe measures {{convert|3|–|10|cm|in|abbr=on}} long by {{convert|1|–|2.5|cm|in|1|abbr=on}} thick, and is either equal in width throughout or tapers slightly towards the base. The stipe is smooth except for a few tufts of hairs near the top. Immediately underneath the [[cap cuticle]], the [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] of the cap is water-soaked; elsewhere in the cap, it is gray-tinted with a sheen, while in the stipe it is dull white. It lacks any appreciable odor or taste.<ref name="Bessette 1995"/> It is considered [[edible mushroom|edible]] in Europe, where it is prized in some regions.<ref name="Overall 2004"/>

The thin-walled, elliptical [[basidiospore|spores]] measure 6.5–8.5 by 4.5–5&nbsp;[[micrometre|μm]]. The [[basidia]] (spore-bearing cells) are narrowly club-shaped, four-spored, and measure 42–55 by 5.4–8.1&nbsp;μm.<ref name="Bessette 1995"/>

==Habitat and distribution==
''Hygrophorus marzuolus'' is a [[mycorrhiza]]l fungus. Associations have been noted with the trees ''[[Abies alba]]'', and sometimes with ''[[Picea excelsa]]'' and ''[[Pinus sylvestris]]''.<ref name="Kraft 1958"/> Fruit bodies grow singly to scattered or sometimes in clusters on the forest floor in [[plant litter|duff]] or in [[moss]]. It fruits under conifers, often [[fir]] and [[Engelmann spruce]], typically at high elevations. A [[snowbank mushroom]], it is associated with melting snow, and fruiting is initiated under snowbanks. The fungus has been recorded from the mountains of [[Idaho]], [[Montana]], [[Oregon]], [[Washington (state)|Washington]], northern [[Arizona]], and northern [[California]].<ref name="Bessette 1995"/> The European distribution extends from the French [[Pyrenees]] to the region of [[Prague]].<ref name="Kraft 1958"/> In Asia, it has been reported from Pakistan.<ref name="Razaq 2005"/>
