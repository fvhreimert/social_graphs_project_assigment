'''''Gymnopilus aurantiophyllus''''' is a species of [[mushroom]]-forming saprotrophic  [[fungus]] in the family [[Hymenogastraceae]].

==Description==
The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{convert|4|to|8|cm|in|1|abbr=on}} in diameter.<ref name="Hesler 1969"/> The caps are a bright yellow orange or [[ochre]].<ref name=":0" /> (Part of the binomial name comes from ''[[List of Latin and Greek words commonly used in systematic names|aurantius]]'' which is Latin for, more or less, orange.) The gills are "narrowly attached with a distinct notch."<ref name=":0">{{Cite book |last1=Siegel |first1=Noah |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jFumDAAAQBAJ&dq=%22Gymnopilus+aurantiophyllus%22&pg=PA133 |title=Mushrooms of the Redwood Coast: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fungi of Coastal Northern California |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |date=2016-08-09 |publisher=Clarkson Potter/Ten Speed |isbn=978-1-60774-817-5 |pages=133 |language=en}}</ref> ''Gymnopilus aurantiophyllus'' has a distinctly bitter taste and is not recommended for eating.<ref name=":0" />

==Habitat and distribution==
''Gymnopilus aurantiophyllus'' has been found growing in clumps on sawdust, in [[Oregon]], in November.<ref name="Hesler 1969"/> It is found in pine forests and on decaying lignin-rich substrates like wood chips or old stumps.<ref name=":0" /> Distribution of this species appears to be primarily the Pacific coast of North America, and it seems to be most frequently observed in the months of December and January.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gymnopilus aurantiophyllus |url=https://www.inaturalist.org/taxa/540875-Gymnopilus-aurantiophyllus |access-date=2024-01-24 |website=iNaturalist |language=en}}</ref>
