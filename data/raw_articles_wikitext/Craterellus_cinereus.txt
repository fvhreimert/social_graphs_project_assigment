'''''Craterellus cinereus''''', commonly known as the '''black chanterelle'''<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/665/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, California |pages=665–66 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> or '''ashen chanterelle''', is a species of fungus in the genus ''[[Craterellus]]''. Found in forests in Europe and North America, it is [[Edible mushroom|edible]].

== Description ==
''Craterellus cinereus'' are greyish-black chanterelle mushrooms with thin, dark grey flesh that fades when dry. The fruiting body can reach {{Convert|12|cm|frac=4}} tall.<ref name="Arora1986" />

The cap is {{Convert|1.5–5|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} across,<ref name="Arora1986" /> in an irregular funnel shape or infundibuliform. It is irregularly wavy at the edges with an inrolled margin. It is black when moist, brownish whey dry or in age.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The stem is 2–8&nbsp;cm long and up to 1.3&nbsp;cm thick.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is smooth to lightly velvety in texture, sometimes with a white woolly base. The veins/ridges are dark grey, irregular forks which are distant and decurrent.

The [[spore print]] is whitish,<ref name="Arora1986" /> while the spores are broadly elliptical, smooth, non-amyloid, and 7.5–10 x 5–6&nbsp;[[μm]]. The taste is mild and the odour indistinct.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Buczacki |first=Stefan |title=Collins fungi guide |date=2012 |publisher=Collins |isbn=978-0-00-724290-0 |location=London |oclc=793683235}}</ref>

=== Similar species ===
Possible lookalikes include ''[[Craterellus cornucopioides]]'',<ref name="Arora1986" /> ''[[Pseudocraterellus undulatus]]'' and ''[[Faerberia carbonaria]],'' all of which are edible.

== Habitat and distribution ==
As a [[Mycorrhiza|mycorrhizal]] species it grows on soil with [[leaf litter]] in broadleaf woods and is found singly or in small groups.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is found in coniferous forests in Europe.<ref>{{cite web |title=Cantharellus cinereus in MycoBank |url=http://www.mycobank.org/Biolomics.aspx?Table=Mycobank&MycoBankNr_=197287}}</ref> It has a widespread distribution but is uncommon, being found in winter and early spring in western North America.<ref name="Arora1986" />

== Edibility ==
''C. cinereus'' is an [[edible mushroom]] with a good taste.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It can be used similarly to black trumpets (''C.&nbsp;cornucopioides'') but with a milder taste.<ref>{{Cite web |last=N |first=gone71 |title=Ashen chanterelle {{!}} Cantharellus cinereus |url=https://www.gone71.com/edible-mushrooms/ashen-chanterelle-cantharellus-cinereus/ |access-date=2022-07-07 |website=Gone71° N |language=de-DE}}</ref>
