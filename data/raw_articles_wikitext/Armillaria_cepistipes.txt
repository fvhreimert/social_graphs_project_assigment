'''''Armillaria cepistipes''''' is a common wood-rotting basidiomycete fungus found in most forests in Central Europe.<ref name="Heinzelmann et al. 2012"></ref> Armillaria cepistipes is a species of [[mushroom]] in the family [[Physalacriaceae]]. This is a weakly [[plant pathogen]]ic species that is typically found growing at the base of [[deciduous trees]] that have previously been stressed by another pathogen.<ref name="Armillaria cepistipes">{{cite web|url=http://imfc.cfl.scf.rncan.gc.ca/maladie-disease-eng.asp?geID=1000059 |title=''Armillaria cepistipes'' |publisher=Natural Resources Canada: Canadian Forest Service |date=2009 |accessdate=2009-04-17 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110706182000/http://imfc.cfl.scf.rncan.gc.ca/maladie-disease-eng.asp?geID=1000059 |archivedate=2011-07-06 }}</ref> The [[mycelium]] of the fungus is [[bioluminescent]].<ref name="Mihail 2015"/>
