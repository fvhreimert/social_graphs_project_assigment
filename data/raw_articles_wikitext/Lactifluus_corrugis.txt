'''''Lactifluus corrugis''''' (formerly '''''Lactarius corrugis'''''), commonly known as the '''corrugated-cap milky''',<ref name="Bessette 2009"/> is an edible species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Russulaceae]].<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |orig-year=2005 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/107 107]}}</ref>

==Taxonomy==
The species was first described by American mycologist [[Charles Horton Peck]] in 1880.<ref name="Peck 1879" />

==Description==
The brownish-red cap is {{Conv|4-12|cm|frac=4}} wide, and is usually dusted by a light bloom (turning dark when touched).<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=187}}</ref> The gills are light yellow and leak white latex,<ref name="audu" /> which stains brown.<ref name="Miller2006" /> The stem is {{Convert|4-13|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} long and {{Convert|1.5-2.5|cm|abbr=on|frac=8}}.<ref name="audu" /> The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="audu" />

It resembles ''[[Lactifluus volemus]]'',<ref name="Miller2006" /> the latex of which also stains brown.<ref name="Miller2006">{{cite book|last1=Miller Jr.|first1=Orson K.|title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi|last2=Miller|first2=Hope H.|publisher=[[FalconGuide]]|year=2006|isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1|location=Guilford, CN|pages=94}}</ref> Additionally, ''[[Lactifluus hygrophoroides|L.&nbsp;hygrophoroides]]'' has a pinkish-orange cap.<ref name="audu" />

== Habitat and distribution ==
The mushroom can be found under [[oak]] trees in eastern North America between July and September.<ref name="audu" />

== Uses ==
''L. corrugis'' is considered a choice [[edible mushroom]].<ref name="Miller2006" />
