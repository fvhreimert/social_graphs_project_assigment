'''''Pseudoboletus parasiticus''''', previously known as '''''Boletus parasiticus''''' and '''''Xerocomus parasiticus''''', and commonly known as the '''parasitic bolete''', is a rare [[Boletaceae|bolete]] mushroom found on ''[[Scleroderma citrinum]]'' earthballs in North America.

==Taxonomy==
''Pseudoboletus parasiticus'' is one of the earliest-diverging lineages of the [[Boletaceae]], after the [[clade]] comprising ''[[Chalciporus]]'' and ''[[Buchwaldoboletus]]''.<ref name="Nuhn2013" />

==Description==
The cap is hemispherical when young,{{Citation needed|date=March 2025}} later flat, yellowish brown or darker and up to {{Convert|6|cm|frac=4}} wide.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=352}}</ref> The yellow stem is {{Convert|3-7|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}} tall and {{Convert|6-15|mm|frac=8}} thick.<ref name="audu" /> The flesh is pale yellow and the [[spore print]] is olive brown.<ref name="audu" />

== Habitat and distribution ==
It can be found found growing on ''[[Scleroderma citrinum]]'' earthballs in eastern North America from July to September.<ref name="audu" />

== Edibility ==
While edible, it is not recommended to do so, due to concerns about being poisoned by its host, which is poisonous.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=255}}</ref>
