'''''Helvella elastica''''', commonly known as the '''flexible Helvella''',<ref name="Tylutki 1979" /> '''elastic saddle''',<ref name="urlRogers Mushrooms"/> or '''brown elfin saddle''',<ref name="Arora 1986" /> is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Helvellaceae]] of the order [[Pezizales]]. It has a roughly saddle-shaped yellow-brown [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] atop a whitish [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]]. It grows on soil in woods in Eurasia and North America. 

== Description ==

The [[ascocarp|fruit body]] of the fungus is grayish or olive-brown, saddle- or mitral-shaped (i.e., resembling a double [[mitre]]) and is attached only to the top of the stipe; it may be up to {{convert|3.5|cm|1|abbr=off|frac=4}} wide.<ref name=":1" /><ref name="Jordan 2004" /> The underside is white.<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=282–283|language=en}}</ref> The [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is white, solid or filled with loosely stuffed [[hypha]]e, has a smooth surface, and is up to {{convert|8|cm|1|abbr=on|frac=8}} long by {{convert|1|cm|1|abbr=on|frac=8}} thick.<ref name="Tylutki 1979"/> The [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] of ''H.&nbsp;elastica'' is brittle and thin. The odor and taste are indistinct.<ref name="Jordan 2004"/>

===Microscopic characteristics===

The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are oblong to elliptical in shape, translucent ([[hyaline]]), contain one central oil drop (''guttulate''), and have dimensions of 18–22 by 10–14&nbsp;[[μm]]; young spores have coarse surface warts, while older ones are smooth. The spore-bearing cells, the [[ascus|asci]], are 260 by 17–19&nbsp;μm. The [[paraphyses]] (sterile cells interspersed between the asci) are club-shaped, filled with oil drops, sometimes branched, and are 6–10&nbsp;μm at the apex.<ref name="Tylutki 1979"/>

=== Similar species ===
The closely related fungus ''[[Helvella albipes]]'' has a thicker stipe and a two- to four-lobed cap.<ref name="Tylutki 1979" /> ''[[Helvella compressa|H.&nbsp;compressa]]'' and ''[[Helvella latispora|H.&nbsp;latispora]]'' have cap edges that are curled upward, rather than inward as in ''H.&nbsp;elastica''.<ref name=":1">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=28}}</ref><ref name="urlme" /> ''[[Helvella maculata|H.&nbsp;maculata]]'' has a similar cap but a ribbed stem. ''[[Gyromitra infula]]'' has an orange and more defined cap.<ref name=":1" />

== Habitat and distribution ==

This fungus is typically found fruiting singly, scattered, or clustered together on the ground or on wood in [[conifer]]ous and [[deciduous]] woods.<ref name="McKnight 1987"/> It has been found in Europe,<ref name="Jordan 2004" /> Japan,<ref name="Nagao 2002"/> China,<ref name="Zhuang 2004"/> and western North America.<ref name="Orr 1979"/> It is present in summer and fall.<ref name=":0" />

== Potential toxicity ==

Consumption of this fungus is not recommended as similar species in the family [[Helvellaceae]] contain the toxin [[gyromitrin]].<ref name="Horgen 1985" />

== Fibrinolytic activity ==

A 2005 Korean study investigated the ability of extracts from 67 different mushroom species to perform [[fibrinolysis]], the process of breaking down [[blood clot]]s caused by the protein [[fibrin]]. ''H.&nbsp;elastica'' was one of seven species that had this ability; the activity of the extract was 60% of that of [[plasmin]], the [[scientific control|positive control]] used in the experiment.<ref name="Kim 2005"/>
