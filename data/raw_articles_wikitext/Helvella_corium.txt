'''''Helvella corium''''' is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Helvellaceae]] of the order [[Pezizales]]. This inedible cup-shaped fungus is black, and grows on the ground often near [[willow]]s in [[deciduous forest|deciduous]] or [[mixed forest]]s.

==Description==
The black [[ascocarp|fruit body]] (technically called an [[apothecium]]) is cup-shaped, covered with either scales or small silk-like surface fibrils (''fibrillose''), and up to {{convert|5|cm|in|1|abbr=off|frac=2}} in diameter. The upper margin of the fruit body cup may be rounded with scalloped or lobed edges (''crenate''). The short, slender [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] (typically {{convert|0.6|to|1.8|cm|abbr=on|frac=4|6=1}} tall)<ref name="Jordan 2004"/> is black on the upper part, but gray at the base; it is cylindrical and tapering (''terete'') with rounded ribs at its base.<ref name="Tylukti 1979"/> The odor and taste are not distinctive.<ref name="Jordan 2004"/>

The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are [[ellipsoid]] in shape, and measure 17–21 by 10–12&nbsp;[[micrometre|μm]]. They are [[hyaline]] (translucent), and contain a single central oil drop (''guttulate'').<ref name="Tylukti 1979"/> The spore-bearing cells, the [[ascus|asci]], are 225–250 by 12–17&nbsp;μm.<ref name="Abbott 1997"/>

=== Similar species ===
''[[Plectania nannfeldtii]]'' is a similar-looking fungus with a black-colored stalked cup, but this species has a longer stipe, up to {{convert|5|cm|in|1}}; microscopically, it has larger spores (typically 23–28 by 11–14&nbsp;μm).<ref name="Evenson 1997" />

==Distribution and habitat==
''Helvella corium'' has been collected from Asia,<ref name="Liu 1985"/> Europe,<ref name="Dissing 1966"/> and North America.<ref name="Abbott 1997"/><ref name="Dissing 1967"/> It is uncommon despite the scope of its distribution.<ref name="Abbott 1997" /> Fruit bodies grow solitary, scattered, or clustered in groups. It is often found in association with the trees ''[[Populus tremuloides]]'' or ''[[Thuja plicata]]'', or with shrubs from genus ''[[Salix]]'' (such as ''[[Salix herbacea]]'' and ''[[Salix glauca]]''), ''[[Shepherdia canadensis]]'' or shrubs from the genus ''[[Dryas (plant)|Dryas]]''.<ref name="Abbott 1997"/> Jordan notes a preference for growing on sandy soils or in dunes.<ref name="Jordan 2004"/>

This mushroom appears to have a high tolerance for otherwise inhospitable growing conditions, as it has been found growing on [[Corrosive substance|caustic]] spoil mounds (the end-product of many [[mining]] and [[manufacturing]] operations) of a [[sodium hydroxide|alkali]] factory in [[Kraków]], Poland,<ref name="Turnau 1991"/> as well as on abandoned [[uranium tailings]] in [[Ontario]], Canada.<ref name="Kalin 1980"/>

== Potential toxicity ==
The edibility of the species is unknown, and its consumption is not recommended as similar species in the family [[Helvellaceae]] contain the toxin [[gyromitrin]].{{cn|date=October 2022}}
