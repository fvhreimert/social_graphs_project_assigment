'''''Gymnopilus braendlei''''' is a [[species]] of [[agaric]] fungus in the family [[Hymenogastraceae]] that contains the [[hallucinogens]] [[psilocybin]] and [[psilocin]].<ref name="worldwide">{{Cite journal |author=Gastón Guzmán  |author-link=Gastón Guzmán  |author2=John W. Allen  |author3=Jochen Gartz|date=1998|title=A worldwide geographical distribution of the neurotropic fungi, an analysis and discussion|url=http://www.museocivico.rovereto.tn.it/UploadDocs/104_art09-Guzman%20&%20C.pdf|journal=Annali del Museo Civico di Rovereto|issue=14|pages=189–280}} (on Fondazione Museo Civico di Rovereto)</ref>  It was originally [[species description|described]] by mycologist [[Charles Horton Peck]] as ''Flammula braendlei'', from specimens found in the [[District of Columbia]] in 1902.

==Description==
*'''[[Pileus (mycology)|Pileus]]''': 2.5–5&nbsp;cm, hemispheric becoming convex, sometimes slightly [[wikt:umbilicate|umbilicate]], [[hygrophanous]], purplish when young then pinkish and lighter towards the margin, becoming yellowish in age with greenish stains, fibrillose, sometimes [[wikt:squamulose|squamulose]] toward the center, flesh whitish, thin, staining greenish.
*'''Gills''': Adnate, sometimes slightly sinuate in attachment, broad, close, whitish when young, becoming bright orangish brown to mustard yellow, becoming bright orangish brown in age.
*'''Spore print''': Orangish brown.
*'''[[Stipe (mycology)|Stipe]]''':  2.5–4&nbsp;cm x 3–4&nbsp;cm thick, more or less equal, pallid, sometimes yellowish at the base, fibrillose above, stuffed or hollow, veil fibrillose, sometimes leaving a silky zone but not forming an [[annulus (mycology)|annulus]].
*'''Taste''': Bitter
*'''Microscopic features''': Spores 6 x 8.5 x 4.5—5 [[μm]] ellipsoid to ovoid in face view, [[dextrinoid]], [[wikt: verruculose|verruculose]], no germ pore. [[wikt: pleurocystidium|Pleurocystidia]] 22—33 x 6—7 [[μm]], cheilocystidia 20—34 x 3—7 [[μm]], no caulocystidia, [[clamp connection]]s present.
*'''Bruising''': Green or blue bruising at the base or on the [[Pileus (mycology)|pileus]], and green spots on pileus likely.

== Distribution and habitat ==
''Gymnopilus braendlei'' is found growing solitary or cespitose on tree stumps from June to November.
It is widespread in the eastern U.S, and present in the western U.S.
