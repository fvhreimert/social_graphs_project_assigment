'''''Clitocybe fragrans''''' is a species of mushroom with several lookalikes, some of which are [[Mushroom poisoning|poisonous]].

== Description ==
It is a white mushroom. The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] ranges from {{Convert|1-5|cm|frac=4}} in diameter.<ref name=":0" /> The gills are somewhat close.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=497}}</ref> The [[stipe (mycology)|stem]] is {{Convert|2-6|cm|abbr=on|frac=8}} long and {{Convert|2–5|mm|frac=16}} wide.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=148–149|oclc=797915861}}</ref> It has a fragrant odour resembling [[aniseed]].<ref name="audu" /> The [[spore print]] is whitish.<ref name="audu" />

=== Similar species ===
Within its genus, it notably resembles the deadly ''[[Clitocybe dealbata]]'' (which lacks the anise odour)<ref name=":0" /> and the edible ''[[Clitocybe odora]]'' (the aniseed toadstool).<ref name=":0" />

Other lookalikes lacking the anise scent can be found especially in the genera ''[[Leucocybe]]'', ''[[Rhizocybe]]'', ''[[Singerocybe]]''.<ref name="audu" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
It can be found throughout Europe growing in [[broadleaf woodland]], from August to December.<ref name="tfb">{{Cite book |last=Francis-Baker |first=Tiffany |title=Concise Foraging Guide |date=2021 |publisher=[[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] |isbn=978-1-4729-8474-6 |series=[[The Wildlife Trusts]] |location=London |pages=136}}</ref> It can also be found in North America's West Coast and [[Pacific Northwest]] regions under conifers, from October to February.<ref name="audu" />

== Edibility ==
While [[edible mushroom|edible]], some guides caution against its consumption as it can be confused with deadly [[Mushroom poisoning|poisonous]] species.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/61 61]}}</ref> It can be preserved by drying.<ref name="tfb" />
