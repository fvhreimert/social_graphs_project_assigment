'''''Lepiota subincarnata''''', commonly known as the '''fatal dapperling'''<ref>{{Cite web |title=fatal dapperling (Lepiota subincarnata) |url=https://www.inaturalist.org/taxa/382931-Lepiota-subincarnata |access-date=2024-02-24 |website=iNaturalist |language=en-US}}</ref> and '''deadly parasol''', is a gilled [[mushroom]] of the genus ''[[Lepiota]]'' in the order [[Agaricales]]. It was first [[species description|described]] scientifically by the Danish mycologist [[Jakob Emanuel Lange]] in 1940.<ref name=Lange1940/> Bon and Boiffard described ''Lepiota josserandii'' in 1974, which turned out to be the same species.

The mushroom's cap is light red to red-brown and cream-colored closer to the margin. The gills are whitish and the flesh is white to pinkish towards the top. The stem may be slightly larger at the base, cream-colored with patches of the cap color. The odor is somewhat fruity and the taste is unpleasant.<ref name=":0">{{cite book |last1=Trudell |first1=Steve |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC |title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Ammirati |first2=Joe |publisher=Timber Press |year=2009 |isbn=978-0-88192-935-5 |series=Timber Press Field Guides |location=Portland, OR |pages=75}}</ref>

The species is found in Eurasia and North America,<ref name="Razaq 2013" /> in woods as well as richly soiled parks.<ref name=":0" /> It is known to contain [[alpha-amanitin]] and consuming this fungus can be potentially lethal.<ref name="Hall2003" />
