'''''Ischnoderma resinosum''''' is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Fomitopsidaceae]]. It is commonly known as the '''resinous polypore''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/573/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=573 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> '''late fall polypore''', or '''benzoin bracket'''.

== Taxonomy ==
The species was originally described as ''[[Boletus]] resinosus'' in 1794 by German botanist [[Heinrich Schrader (botanist)|Heinrich Schrader]]. It has acquired an extensive [[synonym (biology)|synonymy]] in its [[Taxonomy (biology)|taxonomic]] history, having been juggled between several [[genus|genera]]. [[Petter Karsten]] transferred it to ''[[Ischnoderma]]'' in 1879 to give it the name by which it is currently known.<ref name="urlFungorum synonymy: Ischnoderma resinosum" />

== Description ==
This shelf mushroom is {{convert|7–30|cm|0|abbr=on}} across,<ref name="Arora1986" /> velvety, dark red/brown, darkening and forming zones in age.<ref name=":0">{{cite book |last1=Trudell |first1=Steve |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC |title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Ammirati |first2=Joe |publisher=Timber Press |year=2009 |isbn=978-0-88192-935-5 |series=Timber Press Field Guides |location=Portland, OR |pages=260 |language=en}}</ref> The whitish to brownish [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] is watery when young and corky with age.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is sweet smelling. 

''[[Ischnoderma benzoinum]]'' is similar and has darker flesh.<ref name=":0" />

== Habitat and distribution ==
This fungus fruits on [[hardwood]] logs and stumps in autumn.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=245}}</ref> A widely distributed species, it has been recorded from Africa, Asia, Europe, and North America.<ref name="Zhishu 1993" />

==Uses==
Young, soft specimens may be cooked and eaten, but the species becomes hard and unpalatable in age.<ref>{{cite book |last=Meuninck |first=Jim |title=Foraging Mushrooms Oregon: Finding, Identifying, and Preparing Edible Wild Mushrooms |date=2017 |publisher=[[Falcon Guides]] |isbn=978-1-4930-2669-2 |pages=53–54}}</ref>
