'''''Amanita magniverrucata''''', commonly known as the '''pine cone amanita''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/274/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=274–75 |orig-date=1979}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |last=Schwarz |first=Christian |title=Mushrooms of the redwood coast: a comprehensive guide to the fungi of coastal northern California |last2=Siegel |first2=Noah |date=2016 |publisher=Ten Speed Press |isbn=978-1-60774-817-5 |location=Berkeley}}</ref> or '''great pine jewel''', is a species of [[agaric]] mushroom in the family [[Amanitaceae]].

== Taxonomy ==
It was first described scientifically by American mycologists [[Harry Delbert Thiers]] and Joseph Ammirati in 1982.<ref name="Theirs1982" />

== Description ==
It is a whitish mushroom. The cap is typically up to {{Convert|20|cm|frac=2}} wide with dark scales. The gills are very close. The stem is about {{Convert|7–13|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}} long.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/274/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=274–75 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> The smell is mild but unpleasant in age and the [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |page=533}}</ref>
While its edibility is unknown, it may be [[Mushroom poisoning|poisonous]],<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |publisher=Firefly Books |year=2010 |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |location=Buffalo, NY |page=24}}</ref> as are other [[Amanita]]s in the same subgroup.<ref name="Arora1986" />

Although there are a number of lookalike species, its large warts are a distinguishing feature.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/274/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=274–75 |orig-date=1979}}</ref>

== Ecology ==
It is [[mycorrhiza]]l and associates with the tree ''[[Pinus radiata]]'' (the Monterey pine).<ref name="Theirs1982" />
