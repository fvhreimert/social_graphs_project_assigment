'''''Cantharellus cascadensis''''', commonly known as the '''cascade chanterelle''' or '''hybrid chanterelle''',<ref>{{Cite book |last=Siegel |first=Noah |title=Mushrooms of Cascadia: A Comprehensive Guide to Fungi of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |date=September 1, 2024 |publisher=Backcountry Press |isbn=9781941624197 |location=Humboldt County, CA |pages=39}}</ref> is a [[fungus]] native to the [[Pacific Northwest]] region of [[North America]].<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=44–45}}</ref> It is considered a choice [[edible mushroom]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-02-02 |title=Cantharellus cascadensis: Siegel, N. |url=http://dx.doi.org/10.2305/iucn.uk.2021-2.rlts.t195922608a195927578.en |access-date=2023-08-22 |website=IUCN Red List of Threatened Species}}</ref>

==Taxonomy==
Both ''Cantharellus cascadensis'' and ''[[Cantharellus roseocanus|C. roseocanus]]'' were first considered to be varieties of ''[[Cantharellus cibarius|C.&nbsp;cibarius]]'', then of ''[[Cantharellus formosus|C.&nbsp;formosus]]''.<ref name=":0" /> ''C.&nbsp;cascadensis'' was genetically classified as its own species in 2003.<ref name="dun03" />

It is named after the [[Cascade Range]], where it was formally described in 2003.

==Description==
The cap is usually bright yellow with a smooth or wooly surface. The [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is club-shaped to bulbous.<ref name=":0" />

=== Similar species ===
Several other species of chanterelle may be found in western North America:
*''[[Cantharellus californicus|C. californicus]]''
*''[[Cantharellus formosus|C. formosus]]''
*''[[Cantharellus roseocanus|C. roseocanus]]''
*''[[Cantharellus subalbidus|C. subalbidus]]''

Additionally, ''[[Hygrophoropsis aurantiaca]]'', ''[[Chroogomphus]] tomentosus'', and species in the genera ''[[Craterellus]]'', ''[[Gomphus (fungus)|Gomphus]]'', ''[[Omphalotus]]'', and ''[[Polyozellus]]'' may have a somewhat similar appearance to ''C.&nbsp;cascadensis''.
