'''''Gymnopilus fulgens''''' is a species of [[mushroom]] in the family [[Hymenogastraceae]]. It was given its current name by mycologist [[Rolf Singer]] in 1951.<ref>{{cite journal |author=Singer R. |year=1951 |title=The Agaricales in modern taxonomy |journal=Lilloa |volume=22 |page=561}}</ref>

==Description==
The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{convert|0.5|to|2.5|cm|in|1}} in diameter.<ref name="isbn0-945345-39-9">{{cite book |author=Hesler LR. |title=North American Species of ''Gymnopilus'' (Mycologia Memoir Series: No 3) |publisher=Lubrecht & Cramer Ltd |location=Knoxville, Tennessee |year=1969 |pages=63â€“64 |isbn=0-945345-39-9}}</ref>

==Habitat and distribution==
''Gymnopilus fulgens'' grows among moss, on peaty soil, charred [[sphagnum]], or burned-over soil. In the United States, it has been collected in [[Michigan]], but it has also been found in Europe. It fruits from June to September.<ref name="isbn0-945345-39-9"/>
