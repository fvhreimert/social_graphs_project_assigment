'''''Cantharellus amethysteus''''', the '''amethyst chanterelle''', is a [[species]] of [[Cantharellus]] from Europe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mycobank.org/BioloMICS.aspx?TableKey=14682616000000067&Rec=250059&Fields=All|title= Cantharellus amethysteus in MycoBank}}</ref>

== Description ==
''Cantharellus amethysteus'' is an edible<ref>{{Cite web |title=Cantharellus amethysteus, Amethyst Chanterelle mushroom |url=https://www.first-nature.com/fungi/cantharellus-amethysteus.php |access-date=2022-07-07 |website=www.first-nature.com}}</ref> chanterelle species with white flesh which turns yellow as it ages.

'''Cap:''' 5-10cm. Flat topped to start with depression forming. Downy texture with irregular, wavy edges. '''Stem:''' 3-4cm. Tapers downwards, often flattened or twisted. '''Veins/ridges:''' Thick and decurrent. Paler colour than the cap.  '''Taste:''' Mild. '''Smell:''' Mild and pleasant, faint smell of apricots. '''Spore print:''' white to palish yellow sometimes with a pink tinge. '''Spores:''' Cylindrical to elongated ellipsoid, smooth, non-amyloid. 8-10 x 5.5-6 μm.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Buczacki |first=Stefan |title=Collins fungi guide |date=2012 |publisher=Collins |isbn=978-0-00-724290-0 |location=London |oclc=793683235}}</ref>
