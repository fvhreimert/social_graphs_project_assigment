'''''Laccaria amethysteo-occidentalis''''' is a mushroom found under [[conifers]], usually pine, growing alone, scattered or gregariously in western North America.<ref>Muller, 1984.</ref>

==Description==
The mushroom is entirely purple.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=589}}</ref> The cap is {{Convert|1–7|cm|frac=4}} in width.<ref name=mykoweb>{{cite web|url=http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Laccaria_a-o.html|title= California Fungi—''Laccaria amethysteo-occidentalis'' |author=Michael Wood & Fred Stevens|year=2015|access-date=2016-01-21}}</ref> The gills are purple.<ref name="mushroomexpert">{{cite web|url=http://www.mushroomexpert.com/laccaria_amethysteo-occidentalis.html|title= ''Laccaria amethysteo-occidentalis'' |author=Michael Kuo|year=2015|access-date=2016-01-21}}</ref> The stem is up to {{Convert|14|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} long, fibrous, with a whitish [[mycelium]] near the base.<ref name="audu" />

The spores are 7.5–10.5 x 7–16&nbsp;[[μm]], subglobose or broadly elliptical. The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="audu" />

=== Similar species ===
This species is similar to  ''[[Laccaria amethystina|L. amethystina]]''<ref name="audu" /> but differs by occurring in [[hardwood]] forest in eastern North America, rather than coniferous forest; having a smaller sporocarp; and being a lighter purple color.{{Citation needed|date=September 2021}} [[Laccaria bicolor|''L.&nbsp;bicolor'']] is smaller and less purplish; [[Laccaria laccata|''L.&nbsp;laccata'']] has whitish [[mycelium]] at its base.<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=110–111}}</ref> ''[[Cortinarius violaceus]]'' is darker and has a less fibrillose stipe.<ref name=":0" /> ''[[Pseudoomphalina cokeri]]'' is also similar.<ref name="audu" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
It can be found on the ground near the North American West Coast from October to February.<ref name="audu" />

== Uses ==
The mushroom is [[Edible mushroom|edible]].<ref name="Firefly" />
