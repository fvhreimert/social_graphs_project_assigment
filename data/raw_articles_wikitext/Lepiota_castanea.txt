'''''Lepiota castanea''''', commonly known as the '''chestnut dapperling''' or '''petite parasol''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/307/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=307-8 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> is an uncommon, gilled [[mushroom]] of the genus ''[[Lepiota]]'' in the order [[Agaricales]]. It was described by French mycologist [[Lucien Quélet]] in 1881.

It is known to contain [[amatoxin]]s and is potentially deadly [[Mushroom poisoning|poisonous]].

==Description==

The cap is broadly bell-shaped to flat, white with dark red-brown scales; it is up to 3&nbsp;cm in diameter.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The gills are whitish and the stem is typically chestnut brown and up to 8&nbsp;cm long, with an indistinct [[Annulus (mycology)|ring]].<ref name="Arora1986" />

The flesh is whitish,<ref name="Arora1986" /> with a mild taste. The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="Arora1986" />

==Habitat==
It can be found in coniferous and deciduous woodlands, mostly singly or in small groups.<ref name="Arora1986" />

==Toxicity==

Like several other species of the genus ''Lepiota'', it contains potentially fatal [[amatoxin]]s<ref name="Arora1986" /><ref name="Miller2006">{{cite book |last=Miller Jr. |first=Orson K. |title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi |last2=Miller |first2=Hope H. |publisher=[[FalconGuide]] |year=2006 |isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1 |location=Guilford, CN |pages=58}}</ref> which affect the liver.
