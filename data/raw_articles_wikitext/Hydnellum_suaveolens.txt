'''''Hydnellum suaveolens''''', commonly known as the '''fragrant hydnellum mushroom''',<ref name="Arora 1986" /> is a species of [[fungus]]. It has a funnel-shaped [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] that is typically between {{convert|5â€“15|cm|round=0.5|abbr=on}} in diameter. As its name suggests, it has a strong odor of [[anise]] or [[peppermint]].<ref name="Arora 1986"/>

The species is often found beneath [[conifer]]s and is inedible.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |publisher=Firefly Books |year=2010 |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |location=Buffalo, NY |page=323}}</ref> Gas chromatographic-mass spectral analysis of diethyl ether extracts from fresh specimens of this fungi showed {{Nowrap|[[p-anisaldehyde]]}} and coumarin to comprise 30% and 62% respectively of the volatile odor compounds present.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Wood | first1 = William F. | last2 = DeShazer | first2 = D.
