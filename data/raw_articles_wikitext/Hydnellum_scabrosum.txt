'''''Hydnellum scabrosum''''', also called '''bitter tooth''' or '''bitter hedgehog''', is a species of [[tooth fungus]] in the family [[Bankeraceae]].

==Taxonomy==
It was originally [[species description|described]] by Swedish botanist [[Elias Fries]] as ''Hydnum scabrosum'' in 1836.  Finnish mycologist [[Petter Karsten]] moved it to the genus ''[[Sarcodon]]'' in 1881. This species remained as ''Sarcodon scabrosus'' until 2019, when a molecular analysis using nuclear DNA showed that this and 11 other species lay genetically within the genus ''[[Hydnellum]]'', a genus of which harder woody flesh had previously been considered a distinguishing feature from soft-fleshed ''Sarcodon''.<ref name="Larsson 2019"/>

==Description==
The fruit body (mushroom) has a convex to flattened brownish cap up to {{Cvt|12|cm|frac=8}} across that is covered with brown scales. It can be tinged with pink at the margins and darken with age. The mushroom has yellow-brown spines under the cap that are {{Cvt|5|mm|frac=8}} long and {{Cvt|0.3|mm|frac=64}} in diameter. They are decurrent to the stem. The pinkish brown stem is {{Cvt|2–12|cm|1|frac=8}} high and {{Cvt|1–6|cm|1|frac=8}} wide, and has a narrower base that is a characteristic greyish green colour. The flesh is whitish and has a bitter taste, rendering the mushroom inedible. The flesh smells farinaceous or like watermelon when cut.<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=235|language=en}}</ref><ref name="Marcotullio"/>

=== Similar species ===
Within the genus it is most closely related to ''[[Hydnellum fennicum|H.&nbsp;fennicum]]''.<ref name="Larsson 2019" />

''[[Sarcodon imbricatus]]'' is similar, but the scales do not become as prominent in age.<ref name=":0" /> Other similar species include ''[[Hydnellum fennicum|S.&nbsp;fennicus]]'', ''[[Sarcodon rimosus|S.&nbsp;rimosus]]'', ''S.&nbsp;subincarnatus'', and ''[[Hydnellum underwoodii|S.&nbsp;underwoodii]]''.<ref name=":0" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
It is found in association with hardwood forests across Eurasia to Japan, as well as North America.<ref name=Endo2012/>

==Biochemistry==
The species has been investigated for bioactive agents. In 2004, Tsunashi Kamo and colleagues isolated [[diterpenoid]]s with experimental anti-inflammatory activity.<ref name=kamo2004/> Other diterpenoids were shown to stimulate [[nerve growth factor]] (NGF).<ref name=kita1998/> Yet another agent, an alpha-[[pyrone]], was shown to inhibit lettuce seedling growth.<ref name=Endo2012/>
