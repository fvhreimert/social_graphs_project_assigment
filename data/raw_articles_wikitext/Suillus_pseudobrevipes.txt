'''''Suillus pseudobrevipes''''', commonly known as the '''veiled short-stemmed slippery jack''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/500/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=500-501 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> is a species of fungus in the genus ''[[Suillus]]''. It was first described scientifically by American mycologists [[Harry D. Thiers]] and [[Alexander H. Smith]] in 1964.<ref name=Smith1964/>

The cap is {{Convert|5-15|cm|frac=4}} wide and tannish, darkening with age; the margin may have whitish veil remnants.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The pores are yellow and the [[spore print]] is brown.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The stalk is up to 8&nbsp;cm long and 3&nbsp;cm thick. A fibrillous [[Annulus (mycology)|annulus]] is usually present.<ref name="Arora1986" />

It is distributed in North America under [[pine]] trees. The fruit body is [[Edible mushroom|edible]].<ref name="Arora1986" />
