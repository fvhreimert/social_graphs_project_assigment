'''''Peziza arvernensis''''', commonly known as the '''boring brown cup fungus''' or '''fairy tub''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/821/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, California |pages=821 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> is a species of [[apothecia]]l [[fungus]] belonging to the family [[Pezizaceae]]. The [[ascocarp]]s appear as cups, growing up to {{convert|8|cm|abbr=on|frac=8}} across.<ref name="Arora1986" /> Their exterior is pale tan and the interior is brown.<ref name=":0">{{cite book |last1=Trudell |first1=Steve |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC |title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Ammirati |first2=Joe |publisher=Timber Press |year=2009 |isbn=978-0-88192-935-5 |series=Timber Press Field Guides |location=Portland, OR |pages=290–291 |language=en}}</ref> ''[[Peziza vesiculosa]]'' and [[Peziza violacea|''P.&nbsp;violacea'']] are similar, young specimens of the latter having a violet hue.<ref name=":0" />

This species often appears in small groups on soil in [[broad-leaved]] [[woodland]], especially with [[beech]]. It is widespread in Europe with a few records from North and South America.
