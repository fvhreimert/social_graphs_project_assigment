'''''Clavariadelphus truncatus''''', commonly known as the '''truncate club coral''',<ref name=":1" /> '''truncated club''',<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.britmycolsoc.org.uk/library/english-names |title= Clavariadelphus truncatus · truncated club |access-date= 2021-05-21 |work= The British Mycological Society |archive-date= 2022-02-05 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20220205063207/https://www.britmycolsoc.org.uk/library/english-names |url-status= dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://eol.org/pages/190328 |title= Clavariadelphus truncatus · truncated club |access-date=2021-05-21 |work= EOL }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://species.nbnatlas.org/species/NBNSYS0000020722 |title= Clavariadelphus truncatus · truncated club |access-date=2021-05-21 |work= NBN Atlas }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://inaturalist.nz/taxa/55160-Clavariadelphus-truncatus |title= Truncated Club (Clavariadelphus truncatus) |access-date=2021-05-21 |work= Ninaturalist.nz }}</ref> or '''club coral''',<ref>{{cite web|url= https://botany.cz/en/clavariadelphus-truncatus/ |title= Clavariadelphus truncatus (Quél.) Donk – Truncate Club Coral, Truncated Pestle |access-date=2021-05-21 |work= botany.cz }}</ref> is a species of [[mushroom]]. It is a member of the [[basidiomycete]] fungi family [[Gomphaceae]].

==Description==
The species has a yellow-orange [[fruiting body]] in the shape of a club with a flat cap.<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=239|language=en}}</ref> The [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] is white, thin, and hollow at the top.<ref name="Miller 2006">{{cite book|last1=Miller Jr.|first1=Orson K.|title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi|last2=Miller|first2=Hope H.|publisher=[[FalconGuide]]|year=2006|isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1|location=Guilford, CN|pages=343|author-link=Orson K. Miller Jr.}}</ref> The vertical side of the fruiting body normally has folds and wrinkles, but can be smooth. The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are smooth and their [[spore print]] is pale yellow to [[ochre]].<ref>{{cite book | last = C. Roody | first = William | title = Mushrooms of West Virginia and the Central Appalachians | publisher = University Press of Kentucky | year = 2003 | location = Kentucky | pages = 408 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=5HGMPEiy4ykC&q=Clavariadelphus+truncatus&pg=PA408 | isbn = 978-0-8131-9039-6 }}</ref>

The mushroom has a pleasant odor and a sweet taste.<ref name="Miller 2006" />

=== Chemistry ===
''C. truncatus'' can [[bioaccumulate]] significant amounts of [[zinc]],<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Gonen |first1=TF |last2=Yamac M, Cabuk A, Yildiz Z |first2=M |last3=Cabuk |first3=A |last4=Yildiz |first4=Z |year=2008 |title=Selection of newly isolated mushroom strains for tolerance and biosorption of zinc in vitro |journal=Journal of Microbiology and Biotechnology |volume=18 |issue=3 |pages=483–489 |pmid=18388466}}</ref> and radioactive [[caesium-137]].<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Gaso |first1=MI |last2=Segovia N, Morton O, Lopez JL, Machuca A, Hernandez E |first2=N |last3=Morton |first3=O |last4=Lopez |first4=JL |last5=Machuca |first5=A |last6=Hernandez |first6=E |year=2007 |title=Radioactive and stable metal bioaccumulation, crystalline compound and siderophore detection in ''Clavariadelphus truncatus'' |journal=Journal of Environmental Radioactivity |volume=97 |issue=1 |pages=57–69 |doi=10.1016/j.jenvrad.2007.03.003 |pmid=17466420|bibcode=2007JEnvR..97...57G }}</ref>

=== Similar species ===
Although one field guide says that it is unlikely that anyone would confuse the mushroom with another species,<ref>{{cite book|last=Authors|first=Multiple|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zsJN7NZutTAC&q=Clavariadelphus+truncatus&pg=PA114|title=Edible Wild Mushrooms of North America: A Field-To-Kitchen Guide|publisher=University of Texas Press|year=1992|isbn=978-0-292-72080-0|location=Texas|pages=114}}</ref> the [[yellow chanterelle]] is distantly related to the mushroom and looks nearly the same, except for the ridges and cross-veined [[hymenium]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Clavariadelphus truncatus|url=http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Clavariadelphus_truncatus.html|access-date=2010-01-17|work=California Fungi}}</ref> In North America, ''[[Clavariadelphus pallidoincarnatus|C.&nbsp;pallidoincarnatus]]'' (found in the West) and ''[[Clavariadelphus unicolor|C. unicolor]]'' (in the East) are similar, as is ''[[Clavariadelphus ligula|C.&nbsp;ligula]]''.<ref name=":audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=123}}</ref> ''[[Clavariadelphus pistillaris]]'' is also similar, but the top is not flat.<ref name=":0" /> Additionally, ''Macrotyphula fistulosa'' is tall and skinny, and ''[[Neolecta]]'' has a bright yellow head.<ref name=":audu" />

==Habitat and distribution==
The mushroom's [[habitat]] is in [[conifer]]ous forests from summer to autumn.<ref name="Miller 2006"/> The mushroom is a common species.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uoguelph.ca/~gbarron/CORAL/clavdelp.htm |title=Clavariadelphus truncatus |access-date=2010-01-17 |work=University of Guelph }}</ref> The species is found at a high elevation and is widely distributed.<ref>{{cite book | last = Stucky Evenson | first = Vera | title = Mushrooms of Colorado and the Southern Rocky Mountains | publisher = Big Earth Publishing | year = 1997 | pages = 165 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=EAeDeyqZLq0C&q=Clavariadelphus+truncatus&pg=PA165 | isbn = 978-1-56579-192-3 }}</ref>

==Uses==
The mushroom is [[edible mushroom|edible]] and has a sweet taste.<ref name="Miller 2006"/> Old mushrooms may be spongy and soft inside.<ref>{{cite book |last=Smith |first=Alexander Hanchett |author2=Weber, Nancy S. | title = The mushroom hunter's field guide | publisher = University of Michigan Press | year = 1980 | location = Michigan | pages = 79 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=TYI4f6fqrfkC&q=Clavariadelphus+truncatus&pg=RA1-PA79 | isbn = 978-0-472-85610-7 }}</ref> The species is high in [[nutrition]] and can be used for cooking.<ref>{{cite book | last = Persson | first = Olle | title = The chanterelle book | publisher = Ten Speed Press | year = 1998 | pages = 78 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=AGRF3tqnCgsC&q=Clavariadelphus+truncatus&pg=PA78 | isbn = 978-0-89815-947-9 }}</ref> One field guide says that the mushroom is one of the best to eat and has a sweet flavor that is especially appealing to some people.<ref>{{cite book |last1=McKnight |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kSdA3V7Z9WcC&q=Clavariadelphus+truncatus&pg=PA72 |title=A Field Guide to Mushrooms: North America |last2=McKnight |publisher=Houghton Mifflin Harcourt |year=1998 |isbn=978-0-395-91090-0 |pages=72}}</ref> [[David Arora]] writes that the mushroom can be [[sautéed]] and served for dessert.<ref name=":1">{{cite book |last=Arora |author-link=David Arora |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/634/mode/2up |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |location=Berkeley, California |year=1986 |orig-date=1979 |edition=2nd |pages=634}}</ref>

=== Medicine ===
The mushroom contains [[clavaric acid]], which has been shown to reduce the rate of [[tumor]] development when given to mice.<ref name="ACSASP">{{cite journal|title = Note Clavaric Acid: A Triterpenoid Inhibitor of Farnesyl-Protein Transferase from Clavariadelphus truncatus |year=1998 |journal =Journal of Natural Products | doi = 10.1021/np980200c | volume = 61 | issue = 12 | pages = 1568–1570|last1=Jayasuriya|first1=Hiranthi|last2=Silverman|first2=Keith C.|last3=Zink|first3=Deborah L.|last4=Jenkins|first4=Rosalind G.|last5=Sanchez|first5=Manuel|last6=Pelaez|first6=Fernando|last7=Vilella|first7=Dolores|last8=Lingham|first8=Russell B.|last9=Singh|first9=Sheo B.|pmid=9868169}}</ref> Clavaric acid interferes with [[farnesyltransferase]], an enzyme implicated in [[tumorigenesis]], which suggests that clavaric acid may have therapeutic value in the treatment of certain cancers.<ref name="ACSASP" /> It has been reported that the mushrooms have significant [[antioxidant]] activity.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Sevi̇ndi̇k|first=Mustafa|date=2018-10-25|title=Investigation of Oxidant and Antioxidant Status of Edible Mushroom Clavariadelphus truncatus|url=https://dergipark.org.tr/en/pub/mantar/issue/39914/427457|journal=Mantar Dergisi|language=en|volume=9|issue=2|pages=165–168|doi=10.30708/mantar.427457|doi-broken-date=11 July 2025}}</ref>
