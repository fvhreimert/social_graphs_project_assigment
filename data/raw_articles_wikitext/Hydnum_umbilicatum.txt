'''''Hydnum umbilicatum''''', commonly known as the '''depressed hedgehog''',<ref name="McKnight 1987"/> is a species of [[tooth fungus]] in the family [[Hydnaceae]]. It was scientifically [[species description|described]] in 1902 from New York by American mycologist [[Charles Horton Peck]].<ref name="Peck 1902"/> It is found in eastern North America. It is [[edible mushroom|edible]]<ref name="McKnight 1987"/><ref>{{cite book |last=Meuninck |first=Jim |title=Foraging Mushrooms Oregon: Finding, Identifying, and Preparing Edible Wild Mushrooms |date=2017 |publisher=[[Falcon Guides]] |isbn=978-1-4930-2669-2 |page=66}}</ref> and good.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=323}}</ref>

Hydnum umbilicatum has been found to be associated with [[Pine|pines]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stevens|first=Michael Wood & Fred|title=California Fungi: Hydnum umbilicatum|url=http://www.mykoweb.com/CAF/species/Hydnum%20umbilicatum.html|access-date=2021-09-07|website=www.mykoweb.com}}</ref> 

Can be confused with [[Hydnum repandum]], also known as the Wood Hedgehog. These two can be distinguished by their size and distribution, with H. repandum being a much larger species found in Europe.
