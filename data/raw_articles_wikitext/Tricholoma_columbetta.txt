'''''Tricholoma columbetta''''', commonly known as '''dove-coloured tricholoma''', is an [[edible mushroom]]<ref name=Firefly/> of the large genus ''[[Tricholoma]]''. It is found in Europe, where it is eaten in France.

==Genus==
[[Elias Magnus Fries]] described the species in 1821 as ''Agaricus columbetta''.<ref name=fries1821/> [[Paul Kummer]] placed it in the genus ''[[Tricholoma]]'' in 1871, within which it is classified in the Section ''Albata''.<ref name=Kummer1871/>

==Description==
The fruit body (mushroom) is white or ivory-coloured, sometimes with a pale ochre tinge in the centre of the cap or pinkish,<ref name=Bas1999/> violet-blue or greenish spots.<ref name="Phillips 2006"/> The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is conical in young specimens, expanding to convex or flattish with a wavy margin, and is 4–10&nbsp;cm in diameter. It can be a little sticky when wet. The centre of the cap may have a small boss or be depressed. The  [[lamella (mycology)|gills]] are adnate and widely spaced. The cylindrical [[stipe (mycology)|stalk]] is 6&ndash;14&nbsp;cm tall and 0,8&ndash;2&nbsp;cm thick, and has no [[annulus (mycology)|ring]]. The mushroom has a mealy smell, which is stronger when it is cut. The [[spore print]] is white. The spores are 5&ndash;7.5 x 3.5&ndash;5.5 μm.<ref name=Bas1999/> ''Tricholoma columbetta'' is edible, with a pleasant taste.<ref name="Lamaison 2005"/>

''[[Tricholoma albidum]]'' is similar but stains yellow when cut or bruised.<ref name=Bas1999/> ''T. columbetta'' could be confused with paler specimens of the poisonous ''[[Entoloma lividum]]'', though the latter has a more grey-white cap, yellow or pink gills.<ref name="Lamaison 2005"/>

==Distribution==
Widespread across Europe, ''Tricholoma columbetta'' forms mycorrhizal relationships with oak (''[[Quercus]]'') and is found in woodlands, parks, and rarely sand dunes on sandy mildly acidic soils. Mushrooms appear from August to November.<ref name=Bas1999/> In southern Finland, mushrooms appear in August and September.<ref>{{cite book | author = Phillips, Roger  | title = WSOY Suuri Sienikirja | orig-year = 1981 | date= 1992 | page= 43 | publisher = WSOY | isbn = 951-0-17255-3}}</ref>

In 2010, Roger Phillips reported what "seems to be a first record of this species in North America."<ref name=Firefly>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/49 49]}}</ref>
