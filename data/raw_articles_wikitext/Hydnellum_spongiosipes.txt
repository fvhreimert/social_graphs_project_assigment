'''''Hydnellum spongiosipes''''', commonly known as the '''velvet tooth''',<ref name="BMS"/> is a [[tooth fungus]] in the family [[Bankeraceae]]. It is found in Europe and North America. In Switzerland, it is considered a [[vulnerable species]].<ref name="Senn-Irlet 2007"/>

==Taxonomy==
The fungus was originally [[species description|described]] as new to science in 1898 by American mycologist [[Charles Horton Peck]], who placed it in the genus ''[[Hydnum]]''.<ref name="Peck 1897"/> [[Zdeněk Pouzar]] transferred it to ''[[Hydnellum]]'' in 1960.<ref name="Pouzar 1960"/> [[Synonym (biology)|Synonyms]] include ''Hydnellum nuttallii'', published by [[Howard James Banker]] in 1906, and ''Hydnellum velutinum'' var. ''spongiosipes'', published by [[Rudolph Arnold Maas Geesteranus]] in 1957.<ref name="urlFungorum synonymy: Hydnellum spongiosipes"/>

==Description==
The cap is up to {{Convert|8|cm|frac=4}} wide. The flesh is light brown on the outside and darker within. The [[spore print]] is brown.<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=214}}</ref>

=== Similar species ===
Lookalikes include ''[[Hydnellum concrescens|H.&nbsp;concrescens]]'', ''[[Hydnellum ferrugineum|H.&nbsp;ferrugineum]]'', and ''[[Hydnellum scrobiculatum|H.&nbsp;scrobiculatum]]''.<ref name="audu" />

==Distribution==
It is found in Europe and North America.<ref name="Pegler 1997" /> In the latter, it appears in the east from July to September.<ref name="audu" />
