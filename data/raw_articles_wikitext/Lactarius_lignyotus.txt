'''''Lactarius lignyotus''''' ('''chocolate milky''')<ref name=falc /> is a member of the large milk-cap genus ''[[Lactarius (fungus)|Lactarius]]'' in the order [[Russulales]]. It was first [[species description|described]] scientifically by [[Elias Magnus Fries]] in 1855.

The cap is up to {{Convert|4|in|cm|order=flip}} wide and brown. The stalk is brown and white at the base. The flesh oozes a milky latex when split. It has a yellow or ochre [[spore print]].<ref name=falc>{{Cite book |last=Telander |first=Todd |title=Mushrooms |date=2023 |publisher=Falcon Guides |isbn=978-1-4930-6558-5 |edition=2nd |series=A Falcon Field Guide |location=Essex, CT |pages=1}}</ref>

It can be found in [[conifer]]ous woodland in eastern North America.<ref name=falc />
It is considered edible, but of little interest.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |orig-year=2005 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/104 104]}}</ref>
