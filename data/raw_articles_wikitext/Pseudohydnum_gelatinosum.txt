'''''Pseudohydnum gelatinosum''''', commonly known as the '''toothed jelly fungus''', '''cat's tongue''', or '''jelly tooth''',<ref name="BMS" /> is a species of [[fungus]] in the order [[Auriculariales]]. Its common names refer to its [[gelatinous]] consistency and [[hydnoid]] (toothed) undersurface. Found in Eurasia and North America, the mushroom is [[Edible mushroom|edible]].

== Description ==
The gelatinous fruit bodies are whitish to light grayish or tan, {{Convert|2-7|cm|abbr=off|frac=4}} wide and tall, with teeth up to {{Convert|3|mm|frac=16}} long.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/671/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, California |pages=671 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name=":02">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=108}}</ref> It has little to no flavour.<ref name="Arora1986" />

== Taxonomy ==
A [[subspecies]], ''Pseudohydnum gelatinosum'' ssp. ''pusillum'', is found in [[North America]].<ref name="Spirin 2023" /> It is the only toothed jelly fungus known in the region.<ref name=":02" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
The species was thought to be [[Cosmopolitan distribution|cosmopolitan]], but recent [[DNA]] evidence suggests that it is confined to Europe and [[northern Asia]], with superficially similar (but distinct) [[taxa]] elsewhere.<ref name="Zhou 2022" /> ''P.&nbsp;gelatinosum'' grows on dead [[conifer]] wood.<ref name="Roberts 2011" />

The North American species can be found near both coasts, between November– February on the west and July–September in other places.<ref name=":02" /> Western specimens usually have a distinct vertical stalk compared to those in the east.<ref name="Arora1986" />

== Uses ==
The jelly tooth is [[Edible mushroom|edible]], even raw, although can benefit from [[marination]].<ref name="Arora1986" /><ref>{{Cite journal |last1=Stoyneva-Gärtner |first1=M. P. |last2=Uzunov |first2=B. A. |last3=Dimitrova |first3=P. |date=June 15, 2017 |title=Jelly-like algae and fungi used as food in Bulgaria |journal=International Journal of Nutrition and Health Sciences |volume=2 |issue=1 |pages=6–9}}</ref> It is consumed as a [[wild food]] in parts of [[Bulgaria]], [[Russia]], and [[Siberia]].<ref name="Boa 2004" />
