'''''Pseudoclitocybe cyathiformis''''', commonly known as the '''goblet funnel cap''', is a [[species]] of [[fungus]] and the [[type species]] of the genus ''[[Pseudoclitocybe]]''. It is found in North America and Europe.

==Taxonomy==
It was first [[species description|described]] scientifically as ''Agaricus cyathiformis'' by [[Jean Baptiste François Pierre Bulliard]] in 1786, and later transferred to the genus ''Pseudoclitocybe'' by [[Rolf Singer]] in 1956.<ref name="Singer1956" /> It is the [[type species]] of the genus ''[[Pseudoclitocybe]]''.

==Description==
The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is up to {{Convert|8|cm|frac=4}} wide and brown, fading in age. It is initially centrally depressed with an inrolled margin then becomes funnel-shaped.<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/164/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |date=1986 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=164–165 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> The [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are adnate to decurrent and pale, becoming gray to brown. The stem may be slightly lighter than the cap and is up to {{Convert|12|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} long and 1&nbsp;cm thick, sometimes larger at the base.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The [[spore print]] is white.<ref name="Arora1986" />

==Distribution and habitat==
The fungus is found in North America and Europe<ref name="urlRogers Mushrooms" /> (September to December), in woodland soil<ref name="tfb">{{Cite book |last=Francis-Baker |first=Tiffany |title=Concise Foraging Guide |date=2021 |publisher=[[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] |isbn=978-1-4729-8474-6 |series=[[The Wildlife Trusts]] |location=London |pages=132}}</ref> or rotting wood.<ref name="Arora1986" />

==Uses==
The species is reportedly [[Edible mushroom|edible]] and can be dried for preservation,<ref name="tfb" /> but resembles a number of species of unknown edibility.<ref name="Arora1986" />
