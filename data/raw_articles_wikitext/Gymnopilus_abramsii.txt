'''''Gymnopilus abramsii''''' is a species of [[mushroom]]-forming [[fungus]] in the family [[Hymenogastraceae]]. It was first [[species description|described]] by American mycologist [[William Alphonso Murrill|Murrill]] in 1917.<ref name="Murrill 1917" /> The [[Specific epithet (botany)|epithet]] ''abramsii'' commemorates [[LeRoy Abrams]].<ref name="Eggli Newton 2004" />

==Description==
The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{convert|4|to|6|cm|in|1|abbr=on}} in diameter.<ref name="Hesler 1969" />

==Habitat and distribution==
Found in [[California]], ''Gymnopilus abramsii'' grows on soil, and typically fruits in November.<ref name="Hesler 1969" />
