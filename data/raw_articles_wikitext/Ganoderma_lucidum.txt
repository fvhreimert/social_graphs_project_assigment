'''''Ganoderma lucidum''''', commonly known as the '''reishi''', '''varnished conk''', '''lacquered bracket''',<ref name="jarvis-2020">{{cite book |last1=Jarvis |first1=Peter J. |title=The Pelagic Dictionary of Natural History of the British Isles |date=2020 |publisher=Pelagic |location=Exeter |isbn=978-1-78427-194-7}}</ref> or '''ling chih''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/577/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=577-78 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> is a red-colored species of ''[[Ganoderma]]'' with a limited distribution in Europe and parts of China, where it grows on decaying [[hardwood]] trees.

==Taxonomy ==
The history of the ''Ganoderma lucidum'' taxon is tied to the history of ''[[Ganoderma]]'' as a genus. Karsten first described the ''Ganoderma'' in 1881 and included only one species in the genus, ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' (Curtis) Karst.<ref name="Karsten1881">{{cite journal |last1=Karsten |first1=P |title=Enumeratio Boletinarum et Polyporarum Fennicarum systemate novo dispositorum |journal=Revue de Mycologie |year=1881 |volume=3 |issue=9 |pages=16–18 }}</ref> Previously, it was called ''Boletus lucidus'' Curtis (1781) and then ''Polyporus lucidus'' (Curtis) Fr. (1821).<ref name="Karsten1881" /> Patouillard revised Karsten's genus Ganoderma to include all species with pigmented spores, adhering tubes and [[lacquer|laccate]]-crusted [[Pileipellis|cuticles]], which resulted in a total of 48 species classified under the genus ''Ganoderma'' in his 1889 monograph.<ref name="Murrill-1902">{{cite journal |last1=Murrill |first1=William Alphonso |title=The Polyporaceae of North America. I. The Genus Ganoderma |journal=Bulletin of the Torrey Botanical Club |date=1902 |volume=29 |issue=10 |pages=599–608 |doi=10.2307/2478682 |jstor=2478682 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Patouillard |first1=N |title=Le genre Ganoderma |trans-title=The genus Ganoderma |language=French |journal=Bulletin de la Société Mycologique de France |date=1889 |volume=6 |pages=64–80 |id={{NAID|10029646237}} }}</ref>

Despite this recognition of additional species and subsequent discoveries of new ''Ganoderma'' species, such as 17 new North American species identified by Murrill North in 1902,<ref name="Murrill-1902" /><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Murrill |first1=WA |title=Agaricales (Polyporaceae) |journal=North American Flora |year=1908 |volume=9 |pages=1–131 |url=https://www.mykoweb.com/systematics/literature/NAF_Vol9Part1_7.pdf }}</ref> the taxonomy of Ganoderma species has remained chaotic, and the species name ''Ganoderma lucidum'' continues to be used for most ''Ganoderma'' species, including commonly misidentifying [[Ganoderma sichuanense|''Ganoderma sichuanense'' (= ''Ganoderma lingzhi'')]] (also known as reishi mushroom (Japan) or lingzhi/ling chih (China)), the sought-after red ''Ganoderma'' species used in traditional Asian medicine.<ref name="Loyd-2018a">{{cite journal |last1=Loyd |first1=Andrew L. |last2=Richter |first2=Brantlee S. |last3=Jusino |first3=Michelle A. |last4=Truong |first4=Camille |last5=Smith |first5=Matthew E. |last6=Blanchette |first6=Robert A. |last7=Smith |first7=Jason A. |date=16 July 2018 |title=Identifying the 'Mushroom of Immortality': Assessing the Ganoderma Species Composition in Commercial Reishi Products |journal=Frontiers in Microbiology |volume=9 |page=1557 |doi=10.3389/fmicb.2018.01557 |pmc=6055023 |pmid=30061872 |doi-access=free}}</ref> It is important to note that ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' is not a synonym for ''G.&nbsp;sichuanense'' (nor ''G.&nbsp;lingzhi'') and is not in the same clade; based on molecular phylogenetic analyses, ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' is more closely related to North American species ''[[Ganoderma tsugae|G.&nbsp;tsugae]]'' and ''[[Ganoderma oregonense|G.&nbsp;oregonense]]'' than to ''G.&nbsp;sichuanense'', whose sister taxa include ''[[Ganoderma curtisii|G.&nbsp;curtisii]]'' and ''G.&nbsp;ravenelii''.<ref name="Loyd-2018" />

These genetic analyses tested species concept hypotheses to determine how the ''Ganoderma'' taxa are related. One such study<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Moncalvo |first1=Jean-Marc |last2=Wang |first2=Huei-Fang |last3=Hseu |first3=Ruey-Shyang |title=Gene phylogeny of the Ganoderma lucidum complex based on ribosomal DNA sequences. Comparison with traditional taxonomic characters |journal=Mycological Research |date=December 1995 |volume=99 |issue=12 |pages=1489–1499 |doi=10.1016/S0953-7562(09)80798-3 |url=http://ntur.lib.ntu.edu.tw//handle/246246/162549 |url-access=subscription }}</ref> found six major clades among the 29 samples studied. Samples labeled as ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' were found in five of the six clades, showing the extent of the confusion around species identification. Another study<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Hong |first1=Soon Gyu |last2=Jung |first2=Hack Sung |title=Phylogenetic analysis of ''Ganoderma'' based on nearly complete mitochondrial small-subunit ribosomal DNA sequences |journal=Mycologia |date=30 January 2017 |volume=96 |issue=4 |pages=742–755 |doi=10.1080/15572536.2005.11832922 |pmid=21148895 }}</ref> found similar results and also showed that ''G.&nbsp;resinaceum'' from Europe and the North American sample wrongly labeled ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' were sister taxa and were also more closely related to each other than the European ''G.&nbsp;lucidum''.

A 2015 [[phylogenic]] study revealed that the global diversity of the ''Ganoderma'' species included three supported major lineages.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Zhou |first1=Li-Wei |last2=Cao |first2=Yun |last3=Wu |first3=Sheng-Hua |last4=Vlasák |first4=Josef |last5=Li |first5=De-Wei |last6=Li |first6=Meng-Jie |last7=Dai |first7=Yu-Cheng |title=Global diversity of the Ganoderma lucidum complex (Ganodermataceae, Polyporales) inferred from morphology and multilocus phylogeny |journal=Phytochemistry |date=June 2015 |volume=114 |pages=7–15 |doi=10.1016/j.phytochem.2014.09.023 |pmid=25453909 |bibcode=2015PChem.114....7Z }}</ref> These results agree with several of the earlier works focusing mostly on morphology, geography and host preference, but with evidence separating the European and North American taxa.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Taylor |first1=John W. |last2=Jacobson |first2=David J. |last3=Kroken |first3=Scott |last4=Kasuga |first4=Takao |last5=Geiser |first5=David M. |last6=Hibbett |first6=David S. |last7=Fisher |first7=Matthew C. |title=Phylogenetic Species Recognition and Species Concepts in Fungi |journal=Fungal Genetics and Biology |date=October 2000 |volume=31 |issue=1 |pages=21–32 |doi=10.1006/fgbi.2000.1228 |pmid=11118132 }}</ref>

=== Etymology ===
The scientific name, ''Ganoderma lucidum'', uses the genus name, ''Ganoderma'' (derived from Greek ganos/γάνος 'brightness, sheen', hence 'shining' and derma/δέρμα 'skin') combined with ''lucidum'' from [[Latin]] ''lucidus'' 'light, bright, clear'.<ref>{{cite web |date=2022 |title=Lucida |url=https://www.etymonline.com/search?q=lucida |access-date=16 September 2022 |publisher=Online Etymology Dictionary}}</ref>

== Description ==
The caps can grow up to {{Convert|35|cm}} across.<ref name="Arora1986" /> The [[Sporocarp (fungi)|fruiting body]] almost always has a [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] present, which is tawny to russet colored and 1.5 times the diameter of the cap. Context tissue (sterile tissue inside the fruiting body between the pileus crust and the initiation of the tubes) is pink-buff to cinnamon-buff and corky, showing concentric growth zones and no resinous or melanoid deposits.{{citation needed|date=October 2021}} The hymenium displays 4–5 pores per millimetre. Chlamydospores are absent. [[Basidiospore]]s are 8.2–12.1&nbsp;[[μm]] (average 10.7&nbsp;μm) long and 4.8–8.9&nbsp;μm (average 7.1&nbsp;μm) wide, with a spore shape index of 66.2.<ref name="Loyd-2018" />

== Cultivation ==
A 2020 study indicated ways to cultivate it in different types of side-streams and waste,<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Atila |first1=Funda |date=April 2020 |title=Comparative study on the mycelial growth and yield of ''Ganoderma lucidum'' (Curt.: Fr.) Karst. on different lignocellulosic wastes |journal=Acta Ecologica Sinica |volume=40 |issue=2 |pages=153–157 |bibcode=2020AcEcS..40..153A |doi=10.1016/j.chnaes.2018.11.007}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Cortina-Escribano |first1=Marta |last2=Pihlava |first2=Juha-Matti |last3=Miina |first3=Jari |last4=Veteli |first4=Pyry |last5=Linnakoski |first5=Riikka |last6=Vanhanen |first6=Henri |date=15 October 2020 |title=Effect of Strain, Wood Substrate and Cold Treatment on the Yield and β-Glucan Content of Ganoderma lucidum Fruiting Bodies |journal=Molecules |volume=25 |issue=20 |page=4732 |doi=10.3390/molecules25204732 |pmc=7587577 |pmid=33076396 |doi-access=free}}</ref> but this species does not usually produce fruiting bodies as well as in other woods, such as aspen, probably because softwoods contain compounds which are toxic to several fungi, including ''Ganoderma'' spp.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Mata |first1=Gerardo |last2=Pérez-Torres |first2=José Antonio |last3=Medel |first3=Rosario |last4=Pérez-Merlo |first4=Rosalía |last5=Salmones |first5=Dulce |date=25 September 2019 |title=Culture of Pleurotus ostreatus in pine shavings: isolation of strains and evaluation of their productivity |journal=Madera y Bosques |volume=25 |issue=2 |doi=10.21829/myb.2019.2521715 |doi-access=free}}</ref> However, recent studies found that ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' is still able to degrade ''[[Pinus sylvestris]]'' wood, causing a wood delignification but keeping proportion of carbohydrates high,<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Cortina-Escribano |first1=Marta |last2=Barbero-López |first2=Aitor |last3=Kilpeläinen |first3=Petri |last4=Vanhanen |first4=Henri |last5=Haapala |first5=Antti |date=28 October 2024 |title=Degradation of Pinus sylvestris and Populus tremula by laccate Ganoderma species |journal=Holzforschung |volume=78 |issue=10 |pages=581–585 |doi=10.1515/hf-2024-0052}}</ref> opening new doors to softwoods in their utilization in ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' cultivation.

== Uses ==
The species is inedible and rock-hard when dried, but is used to make a bitter-tasting tea, purported to have health effects by some cultures, although there is no conclusive scientific evidence for such effects.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Davis |first1=Mike |last2=Sommer |first2=Robert |last3=Menge |first3=John |chapter=Ganoderma lucidum |page=349 |chapter-url={{GBurl|obp7jddvjt4C|p=349}} |title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America |date=2012 |publisher=University of California Press |isbn=978-0-520-95360-4 |oclc=797915861 }}</ref>

It has safety concerns and moderate interactions with certain [[Medication|medications]] and [[Dietary supplement|dietary supplements]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 August 2022 |title=Reishi Mushroom |url=https://medlineplus.gov/druginfo/natural/905.html |access-date=2025-05-19 |publisher=MedlinePlus, U.S. National Library of Medicine}}</ref>

Due to the poor quality of [[clinical research]], there is no good evidence for the use of ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'' in treating [[cardiovascular disease]]s in people with [[type 2 diabetes]] or any other medical condition.<ref name="drugs">{{cite web |date=12 December 2019 |title=Reishi mushroom |url=https://www.drugs.com/npp/reishi-mushroom.html |access-date=4 March 2020 |publisher=Drugs.com}}</ref><ref name="klupp">{{cite journal |last1=Klupp |first1=Nerida L. |last2=Chang |first2=Dennis |last3=Hawke |first3=Fiona |last4=Kiat |first4=Hosen |last5=Cao |first5=Huijuan |last6=Grant |first6=Suzanne J. |last7=Bensoussan |first7=Alan |date=2015 |title=''Ganoderma lucidum'' mushroom for the treatment of cardiovascular risk factors |journal=[[The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews]] |volume=2021 |issue=2 |article-number=CD007259 |doi=10.1002/14651858.CD007259.pub2 |pmc=6486141 |pmid=25686270}}</ref> 

===Products===
]]
The confusion surrounding the taxonomy of ''Ganoderma'' species has persisted, causing confusion and inaccuracies when labeling [[traditional medicine|folklore]] products containing ''Ganoderma'' species, as well as "grow your own" (GYO) kits and other tissue samples sold for cultivation of ''Ganoderma'' species.<ref name="Loyd-2018"/> Products typically carry a label of ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'', using the words "reishi" and "lingzhi/ling chih" (which most typically refer to Asian ''Ganoderma'' species used in traditional medicine, such as ''G.&nbsp;sichuanense'' and ''[[Ganoderma sinense]]'') merely because they contain a laccate ''Ganoderma'' species.<ref name="Loyd-2018"/>

These products and GYO kits sold as ''Ganoderma lucidum'' may not contain ''G.&nbsp;lucidum'': one study showed through [[Genetic testing|DNA analysis]] that 93% of GYO kits and half of the dried mushroom products studied that were labeled "''G.&nbsp;lucidum''" contained ''G.&nbsp;sichuanense'' in actuality,<ref name="Loyd-2018a"/> an inaccurate labeling. The study also found that no manufactured reishi product and only one GYO kit actually contained ''G.&nbsp;lucidum''. Other species present in these products included ''[[Ganoderma applanatum]]'', ''[[Ganoderma australe]]'' (potentially a [[species complex]]), ''Ganoderma gibbosum'', ''[[Ganoderma sessile]]'', and ''G.&nbsp;sinense''.<ref name="Loyd-2018a" />

== Phytochemical composition ==
Garnoderma lucidiums active components include [[ganoderic acid]], alkaloids, flavonoids and saponin which is used for antimicrobial and anti Malaria treatment{{fact|date=March 2025}}
