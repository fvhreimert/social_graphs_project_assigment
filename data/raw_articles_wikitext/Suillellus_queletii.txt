'''''Suillellus queletii''''' (formerly '''''Boletus queletii'''''), commonly known as  the '''deceiving bolete''', is an uncommon, [[edible mushroom]] in the genus ''[[Suillellus]]''.

==Naming==
Originally [[species description|described]] by [[Stephan Schulzer von Müggenburg]] in 1885 as a species of ''[[Boletus]]'', the fungus was transferred to ''[[Suillellus]]'' in 2014.  The epithet ''queletii'' was given in honour of [[Lucien Quélet]].<ref name="Schulzer 1885"/><ref name="Vizzini 2014"/>

In 1796 [[Christiaan Hendrik Persoon]] described a type of [[bolete]] under the name ''Boletus erythropus'' and in the next 200 years the same name was widely used for a well-known species having red pores.  But recently it was discovered that Persoon's mushroom had orange pores, that usage of the name ''B. erythropus'' was invalid, and now the red-pored species (after a separate change to the [[genus]] too) has to be called ''[[Neoboletus luridiformis]])''.  It is best not to use the ambiguous name ''Boletus erythropus'' any more, but according to Funga Nordica the fungus described by Persoon was actually ''Suillellus queletii''.<ref name="FungaNordica"/><ref name=SF/><ref name=SF3/>

==Description==
The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] is hemispherical, latter flattening out, appearing olive to reddish-brown, rarely also dark red.  The [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] is yellow, turning blue when cut.  The pores are initially yellow, soon become orange, and finally can be reddish.<ref name=Eyssartier/>  The [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is smooth and golden yellow and the flesh has a slightly acrid taste.<ref name="Garnweidner 1994"/>

The [[spore]] powder is olive and under the microscope the spores appear somewhat spindle-shaped and measure about 10-14&nbsp;μm × 5-7&nbsp;μm.<ref name=Eyssartier/><ref name="FungaNordica"/>

==Distribution and habitat==
This [[mycorrhizal]] mushroom can be found in [[deciduous forest]], at lower altitudes, under [[beech]], [[oak]] or [[hazel]]. <ref name=Eyssartier/><ref name="FungaNordica"/>

It is uncommon in Europe and considered [[endangered species|endangered]] in the Czech Republic.<ref name="Mikšik 2012"/>  In Asia, it has been recorded in Taiwan.<ref name="Yeh 1980"/>
