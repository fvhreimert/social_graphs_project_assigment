'''''Ganoderma applanatum''''' (the '''artist's bracket''', '''artist's conk''',<ref name="Ginns-2017">{{Cite book|last=Ginns|first=James|title=Polypores of British Columbia (Fungi: Basidiomycota)|publisher=Province of British Columbia - Forests, Lands, and NR Operations|year=2017|isbn=978-0-7726-7053-3|location=Victoria, BC|page=105}}</ref> '''artist's fungus<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/576/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=576–77 |orig-date=1979}}</ref>''' or '''bear bread''') is a [[bracket fungus]] with a [[cosmopolitan distribution]]. As its common names suggest, it can be used as a drawing medium.

==Description==
''Ganoderma applanatum'' is [[parasitic]] and [[saprophytic]],<ref name="Goldman-2019">{{Cite book |last=Goldman |first=Gary B. |title=Field Guide to Mushrooms & Other Fungi in South Africa |date=2019 |publisher=Struik Nature |others=Marieka Gryzenhout |isbn=978-1-77584-654-3 |location=Cape Town, South Africa |oclc=1117322804}}</ref> and grows as a [[mycelium]] within the wood of living and dead trees. It grows in single, scattered, or compound formations.<ref name="Goldman-2019"/> It forms fruiting bodies that are {{Convert|3–30|cm|abbr=off|frac=2}} wide, {{Convert|5–50|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}} long and {{Convert|1–10|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}} thick;<ref name="Ginns-2017"/> exceptionally it may grow up to {{Convert|75|cm}} across.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It is hard as leather and woody-textured.<ref name="Meuninck-2017">{{cite book|last=Meuninck|first=Jim|title=Foraging Mushrooms Oregon: Finding, Identifying, and Preparing Edible Wild Mushrooms|date=2017|publisher=[[Falcon Guides]]|isbn=978-1-4930-2669-2|page=46}}</ref> The upper surface of the fruiting body appears brown,<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |page=272}}</ref> covered with reddish-brown [[conidia]]. The underside is white but stains brown.<ref name="audu" />

The fruiting bodies are [[perennial]], and may persist for multiple years, increasing in size and forming new layers of pores as they grow. These layers can be distinguished in a cross section or from observation of the concentric rings on the upper surface of the fruiting body.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Atkinson |first=George Francis |url=http://archive.org/details/studiesofamerica00atki |title=Studies of American fungi. Mushrooms, edible, poisonous, etc. |date=1901 |publisher=Andrus & Church |location=Ithaca, NY |doi=10.5962/bhl.title.120494}}</ref> This allows the fruiting body's age to be determined using the same method as tree rings.{{Citation needed|date=January 2024}}

Brown [[Basidiospore|spores]] are released from the pores on the underside of the fruiting body. The spores are highly concentrated, and as many as 4.65 billion spores can be dispersed from a {{Convert|10|cm|abbr=on|frac=2}} by 10&nbsp;cm section of the conk within 24 hours.<ref name="usda">{{cite web|url=https://www.fs.usda.gov/Internet/FSE_DOCUMENTS/stelprdb5320331.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170303045242/https://www.fs.usda.gov/Internet/FSE_DOCUMENTS/stelprdb5320331.pdf|archive-date=March 3, 2017|title=White Mottled Rot|website=Fs.usda.gov|access-date=2022-03-21}}</ref> The tubes are {{Convert|4–12|mm|frac=8}} deep and terminate in pores that are round with 4–6 per millimetre.<ref name="Ginns-2017" /> The [[spore print]] is reddish brown.<ref name="audu" />

=== Similar species ===
The similar ''[[Ganoderma brownii]]'' has thicker, darker flesh, often a yellow pore surface, and larger spores than ''G.&nbsp;applanatum.''<ref name="Ginns-2017"/> ''[[Ganoderma oregonense|G.&nbsp;oregonense]]'', ''[[Ganoderma lucidum|G.&nbsp;lucidum]]'',<ref name="Goldman-2019"/> and ''[[Fomitopsis pinicola]]'' are also similar.<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|page=348|oclc=797915861}}</ref> ''[[Fomes fasciatus]]'' produces a white spore print.<ref name="audu" />

== Ecology ==
''G. applanatum'' is a [[wood-decay fungus]], causing a rot of [[heartwood]] in a variety of trees. It can also grow as a [[pathogen]] of live [[Sapwood (wood)|sapwood]], particularly on older trees that are sufficiently wet. It is a common cause of decay and death of [[beech]] and [[Populus|poplar]], and less often of several other tree genera, including [[alder]], [[Malus|apple]], [[elm]], [[Aesculus|buckeye and horse chestnut]], [[maple]], [[oak]], [[live oak]], [[walnut]], [[willow]], [[western hemlock]], [[Douglas fir]], old or sick [[olive tree]], and [[spruce]]. ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' grows more often on dead trees than living ones.<ref name="Biek-1984">{{Cite book |last=Biek |first=David |title=The mushrooms of northern California |date=1984 |publisher=Spore Prints |isbn=0-9612020-0-9 |location=Redding, CA |oclc=10870632}}</ref>

There are anecdotal references of higher primates consuming this fungus for self-medication.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=COUSINS |first1=Don |last2=HUFFMAN |first2=Michael A. |date=June 2002 |title=Medicinal Properties in the Diet of Gorillas: An Ethno-Pharmacological Evaluation |url=http://repository.kulib.kyoto-u.ac.jp/dspace/bitstream/2433/68214/1/ASM_23_65.pdf |journal=African Study Monographs |volume=23 |issue=2 |page=71 |doi=10.14989/68214 |s2cid=52244011}}</ref><ref name="Goldman-2019" /> In her book ''[[Gorillas in the Mist (book)|Gorillas in the Mist]]'' (1983), [[Dian Fossey]] wrote:

<blockquote>Still another special food (for the gorillas) is bracket fungus (''Ganoderma applanatum'')... The shelflike projection is difficult to break free, so younger animals often have to wrap their arms and legs awkwardly around a trunk and content themselves by only gnawing at the delicacy. Older animals who succeed in breaking the fungus loose have been observed carrying it several hundred feet from its source, all the while guarding it possessively from more dominant individuals' attempts to take it away. Both the scarcity of the fungus and the gorillas' liking of it cause many intragroup squabbles, a number of which are settled by the [[silverback]], who simply takes the item of contention for himself.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Fossey |first1=Dian |title=Gorillas in the Mist |date=1983 |publisher=Mariner Books |isbn=978-0-395-28217-5 |page=52}}<!--|access-date=8 March 2016--></ref></blockquote>

 (black objects) of ''[[Agathomyia wankowiczii]]'' on an artist's bracket fungus]]
The midge ''[[Platypezidae|Agathomyia wankowiczii]]'' lays its eggs on the fruiting body of the fungus, forming [[plant gall|galls]].<ref name="SpoonerRoberts2005">{{cite book |author1=Brian Spooner |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_l_wAAAAMAAJ |title=Fungi |author2=Peter Roberts |date=1 April 2005 |publisher=Collins |isbn=978-0-00-220152-0 |page=126 |access-date=31 January 2012}}</ref> Female forked fungus beetles, ''[[Bolitotherus cornutus]]'' lay their eggs on the surface of the fruiting bodies and the larvae live inside of the fruiting bodies of ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' and a few other [[bracket fungi]].<ref name="Liles">{{cite journal |last1=Liles |first1=M.P. |date=1956 |title=A study of the life history of the forked fungus beetle, Bolitotherus cornutus |journal=Ohio J Sci |volume=56 |pages=329–337 |hdl=1811/4397/V56N06_329.pdf}}</ref> Meanwhile, the fly ''[[Hirtodrosophila mycetophaga]]'' courts and mates entirely on the underside of dark fungi.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Evolutionary Biology of Colonizing Species - PDF Free Download |url=https://epdf.pub/the-evolutionary-biology-of-colonizing-species074123ee305e1169d74a87ccd0c5537469973.html |access-date=2019-11-18 |website=Epdf.pub |language=en}}</ref>

==Uses==
 of ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'']]

A peculiarity of this fungus lies in its use as a drawing medium for artists.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-01-12|title=Ganoderma applanatum: The Artist's Conk |website=The Fungal Kingdom |url=http://blogs.evergreen.edu/fungalkingdom/ganoderma-applanatum-the-artists-conk/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150112034350/http://blogs.evergreen.edu/fungalkingdom/ganoderma-applanatum-the-artists-conk/ |archive-date=2015-01-12 |access-date=2021-10-27}}</ref> When the fresh white pore surface is rubbed or scratched with a sharp implement, dark brown tissue under the pores is revealed, resulting in visible lines and shading that become permanent once the fungus is dried. This practice is what gives ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' its common name.<ref name="usda"/><ref name="Biek-1984"/>

''G. applanatum'' is a medicinal farming crop that is used as a flavor enhancer in Asian cuisine. ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' is non-digestible in its raw form<ref name="Goldman-2019"/><ref name="Meuninck-2017" /> due to its toughness.<ref name="Arora1986" /> Hot herbal soups, or fermentation in lemon acid with onion is a common use for cooking with ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' slices as an [[umami]] flavor enhancer in fermented foods. ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' can also be used in tea.{{Citation needed|date=January 2024}}

''G. applanatum'' has been used to produce [[amadou]], although ''[[Fomes fomentarius]]'' is most commonly associated with its production.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sundström|first=Lisa|title=Fnöske - Naturhistoriska riksmuseet|url=https://www.nrm.se/faktaomnaturenochrymden/vaxter/kryptogamer/manadenskryptogam/svampar/fnosktickan/fnoske.1097.html|access-date=2021-11-08|website=Nrm.se|language=sv}}</ref> Amadou is a leathery, easily flammable material that is produced from different [[polypore]]s, but can also be consist of similar material.<ref name="SAOB">{{Cite web|title=fnöske {{!}} SAOB|url=https://www.saob.se/artikel/?seek=Fn%C3%B6ske|access-date=2021-11-08|language=sv-SE}}</ref> Amadou generally has three areas of use: fire making, medicinal,<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Molitoris|first=H. P.|date=1994-04-01|title=Mushrooms in medicine|journal=Folia Microbiologica|language=en|volume=39|issue=2|pages=91–98|doi=10.1007/BF02906801|pmid=7959435|s2cid=19354468|issn=1874-9356}}</ref> and clothing,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sundström|first=Lisa|title=Fnösktickan, Fomes fomentarius, och dess användningsområden|url=https://www.nrm.se/faktaomnaturenochrymden/vaxter/kryptogamer/manadenskryptogam/svampar/fnosktickan.1893.html|access-date=2021-11-08|website=Nrm.se|language=sv}}</ref><ref name="SAOB" /> however, it is mostly associated with fire making.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Göra eld med fnöske|url=http://www.bioresurs.uu.se/bilagan/pdf/xBilagan_2008_eld_fnoske.pdf|url-status=live|archive-date=21 December 2014|website=Bioresurs.uu.se|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141221095142/http://www.bioresurs.uu.se/bilagan/pdf/xBilagan_2008_eld_fnoske.pdf}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Nationalencyklopedin 1991|year=1991|page=450|language=Swedish}}</ref>

=== Medicinal uses ===
[[Medicinal uses of fungi|Medicinal uses]] of ''G. applanatum'' have been extensive over thousands of years.<ref name="Elkhateeb-2018">{{Cite journal|last1=Elkhateeb|first1=Waill A.|last2=Zaghlol|first2=Gihan M.|last3=El-Garawani|first3=Islam M.|last4=Ahmed|first4=Eman F.|last5=Rateb|first5=Mostafa E.|last6=Abdel Moneim|first6=Ahmed E.|date=2018-05-01|title=Ganoderma applanatum secondary metabolites induced apoptosis through different pathways: In vivo and in vitro anticancer studies|url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0753332217369263|journal=Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy|language=en|volume=101|pages=264–277|doi=10.1016/j.biopha.2018.02.058|pmid=29494964|s2cid=4639322 |issn=0753-3322|url-access=subscription}}</ref> In [[Chinese medicine]], the fungus has been used to treat rheumatic tuberculosis and [[esophageal carcinoma]]. It has also been used more commonly to resolve indigestion, relieve pain and reduce phlegm.<ref>{{Cite journal|journal=Chemotherapy|doi=10.1159/000094866|title = Investigation of Potential Antibacterial Properties of Methanol Extracts from Fungus ''Ganoderma applanatum''|year = 2006|last1 = Moradali|first1 = Mohammad-Fata|last2 = Mostafavi|first2 = Hossien|last3 = Hejaroude|first3 = Ghorban-Ali|last4 = Tehrani|first4 = Abbas Sharifi|last5 = Abbasi|first5 = Mehrdad|last6 = Ghods|first6 = Shirin|volume = 52|issue = 5|pages = 241–244|pmid = 16899973|s2cid = 24881421}}</ref> Further studies have shown that its medicinal qualities also include anti-tumor, anti-oxidation and as a regulator for body immunity.<ref name="Elkhateeb-2018" />

''G. applanatum'' is generally studied from three angles: medicinal, phytopathological, and biotechnological.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Ćilerdžić|first1=Jasmina|last2=Stajić|first2=Mirjana|last3=Vukojević|first3=Jelena|date=2016-10-01|title=Degradation of wheat straw and oak sawdust by Ganoderma applanatum|url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0964830516301834|journal=International Biodeterioration & Biodegradation|language=en|volume=114|pages=39–44|doi=10.1016/j.ibiod.2016.05.024|bibcode=2016IBiBi.114...39C |issn=0964-8305|url-access=subscription}}</ref> Medicinal fungi such as ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' are of special interest due to their antibiotic properties. Methanol extracts from ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' have shown that the [[fatty acid]]s present, such as [[palmitic acid]], show antibacterial properties.<ref name="Moradali M">Moradali M, -F, Mostafavi H, Hejaroude G, -A, Tehrani A, S, Abbasi M, Ghods S: Investigation of Potential Antibacterial Properties of Methanol Extracts from Fungus <nowiki><i>Ganoderma applanatum</i></nowiki>. Chemotherapy 2006;52:241-244. doi: 10.1159/000094866</ref> Compared to synthetic antibiotics these compounds extracted from ''G.&nbsp;applanatum'' lack problems of drug resistance and side effects.<ref name="Moradali M" />
