'''''Cortinarius collinitus''''', commonly known as the '''belted slimy cortinarius''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/431/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |pages=431-32 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Cortinariaceae]].

==Description==
The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{Convert|3–10|cm|frac=4}} in diameter,<ref name="Arora1986" /> convex to flat in shape, with a sticky, gelatinous surface (in moist conditions). The gills are [[wikt:adnexed|adnexed]], close, and pallid or pale violet in color. The [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is typically 5–15&nbsp;cm long, 0.5–2&nbsp;cm thick, solid and equal.<ref name="Arora1986" /> It has transverse scaly-looking bands. The [[spore print]] is rusty-brown,<ref name="Arora1986" /> like that of most ''Cortiniarius'' species.

The species is considered inedible,<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=160}}</ref> partly due to the complex of related species not yet having been tested.<ref name="Arora1986" />

== Habitat and distribution ==
It grows singly or in groups with hardwoods and conifers.<ref name="Arora1986" />
