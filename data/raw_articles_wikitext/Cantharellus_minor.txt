'''''Cantharellus minor''''' is a [[fungus]] native to eastern North America. It is one of the smallest of the genus ''[[Cantharellus]]'', which includes other edible [[chanterelle]]s.<ref name="mexp" />

== Description ==
''Cantharellus minor'' is colored bright yellow to yellowish-orange.<ref name=":02">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=98}}</ref> The [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] ranges from {{convert|0.5|to|3|cm|frac=4}} wide and is convex and [[umbo (mycology)|umbonate]], often shallowly depressed, becoming funnel-shaped in some. The yellowish [[lamella (mycology)|gills]] are [[decurrent]], fade to yellowish white in maturity,<ref name="Mohanan2011" /> and may seem large in proportion to the small fruiting body.<ref name=":02" /> The [[stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is {{convert|2-5|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} tall and {{Convert|3-6|mm|frac=8}} thick.<ref name=":02" />

=== Similar species ===
Lookalikes include the [[Gulf Coast]]'s ''[[Cantharellus tabernensis|C. tabernensis]]'' which has a darker center, ''[[Craterellus ignicolor]]'' which has shallower ridges and usually a depression in the cap, and ''[[Gloioxanthomyces nitidus]]'' which has a very circular margin, fairly straight stem and non-forking gills.<ref name=":02" />

== Distribution and habitat ==
Native to eastern North America,<ref name="mexp" /> the fungi fruits from June to September.<ref name=":02" /><ref name="mill06" />

It is suspected of being [[mycorrhiza]]l, found in association with [[oak]]s and [[moss]].<ref name="mexp" />  Recently, ''C.&nbsp;minor'' has been reported from semi-evergreen to evergreen forests in the [[Western Ghats]], [[Kerala]], India forming [[ectomycorrhiza]]l associations with tree species like ''[[Vateria indica]]'', ''[[Diospyros malabarica]]'', ''[[Hopea parviflora]]'', and ''[[Myristica]]'' species.<ref name="Mohanan2011" />

== Uses ==
Although insubstantial, the mushrooms are [[edible mushroom|edible]].<ref name="rogers" />
