'''''Morchella tomentosa''''', commonly called the '''gray''', '''fuzzy foot''', or '''black foot morel''', is a species of [[fungus]] in the family [[Morchellaceae]].<ref name=mexp/> ''M. tomentosa'' is a [[fire ecology|fire-associated]] species described from western [[North America]], formally described as new to science in 2008.<ref name=Kuo2008/>

''Morchella tomentosa'' is identified by its post-fire occurrence, [[tomentose|fine hairs]] on the surface of young fruit bodies, and a thick, "double-walled" stem.<ref name=Kuo2008/><ref name=far05/> It also has unique [[sclerotium|sclerotia]]-like underground parts.<ref name=Stefani2010/>  Color can range from black and "sooty" to gray, brown, yellow, or white, although color tends to progress from darker to lighter with age of the [[ascocarp|fruiting body]].<ref name=mexp/>  Three other wildfire-adapted morels were described from western North America in 2012: ''[[Morchella capitata|M. capitata]]'', ''[[Morchella septimelata|M. septimelata]]'', and ''[[Morchella sextelata|M. sextelata]]''.  None of these three new species share the hairy surface texture of ''M. tomentosa''.<ref name=Kuo2012/>

==Phylogeny==
Based on studies of [[molecular phylogenetics|DNA]], ''M.&nbsp;tomentosa'' is clearly a distinct species apart from the yellow morels (''[[Morchella esculenta|M.&nbsp;esculenta]]'' & ssp.) and black morels (''[[Morchella elata|M.&nbsp;elata]]'' & ssp.).<ref name=Stefani2010/>  Mushroom collectors also use the common name "gray morel" for ''M.&nbsp;esculenta''-type morels in eastern North America.<ref name=mexp/>
