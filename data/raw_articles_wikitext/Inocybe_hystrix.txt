'''''Inocybe hystrix''''' is an [[agaric]] fungus in the family [[Inocybaceae]]. It forms [[mycorrhiza]] with surrounding [[deciduous]] trees. [[Basidiocarp|Fruit bodies]] are usually found growing alone or in small groups on leaf litter during autumn months. Unlike many ''[[Inocybe]]'' species, ''Inocybe hystrix'' is densely covered in brown scales, a characteristic that aids in identification. The mushroom also has a spermatic odour that is especially noticeable when the mushroom is damaged or crushed.

Like many other ''Inocybe'' mushrooms, ''Inocybe hystrix'' contains dangerous amounts of [[muscarine]] and should not be consumed.

==Taxonomy==

The species was first described in 1838 by [[Elias Fries]] under the name ''Agaricus hystrix''.<ref name="Fries 1838"/> Finnish mycologist [[Petter Karsten]] later (1879) transferred it to ''Inocybe''.<ref name="Karsten 1879"/>

==Description==

Fruit bodies have convex to plano-convex caps measuring {{convert|3|–|5.5|cm|in|abbr=on}} in diameter. The caps are dry with scales that can be either erect or flat on the surface. The colour is brown in the centre, becoming paler towards the edges. The [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] is white, and has a spermatic odour and mild taste. The [[lamella (mycology)|gills]] are closely spaced, white to dull brown, and have fringed edges. The stipe measures {{convert|3|–|9|cm|in|abbr=on}} long by {{convert|0.5|–|1|cm|in|1|abbr=on}} thick, and is roughly the same width throughout its length; like the cap, it is scaly.<ref name="Halling 2005"/>

The [[spore print]] is cinnamon brown. [[Basidiospore|spores]] are roughly almond-shaped, smooth, [[inamyloid]], and measure 8–12.5 by 5–6.5&nbsp;[[micrometre|μm]]. [[Clamp connection]]s are present in the [[hypha]]e.<ref name="Halling 2005"/>

The species is poisonous.<ref name="Miller 2006">{{cite book|last=Miller Jr.|first=Orson K.|title=North American Mushrooms: A Field Guide to Edible and Inedible Fungi|last2=Miller|first2=Hope H.|publisher=[[FalconGuide]]|year=2006|isbn=978-0-7627-3109-1|location=Guilford, CN|pages=305|author-link=Orson K. Miller Jr.}}</ref>

==Habitat and distribution==

In North America and Europe, ''Inocybe hystrix'' grows in [[deciduous forest]], especially [[beech]].<ref name="urlRogers"/> In Costa Rica, it is found in the [[Cordillera Talamanca]], where it associates with ''[[Quercus costaricensis]]'' at elevations around {{convert|3000|m|sp=us}}.<ref name="Halling 2005"/>
