'''''Agaricus subrutilescens''''', also known as the '''wine-colored agaricus''', is a [[mushroom]] of the genus ''[[Agaricus]]''.

==Taxonomy==
The species was first described scientifically in 1925 as ''Psalliota subrutilescens''.<ref name=Kauffman1925/> It was transferred to ''Agaricus'' in 1938.<ref name=Hotson1938/>

==Description==
''Agaricus subrutilescens'' has a [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] that is {{convert|5–15|cm|abbr=on|round=0.5}} across, dry, and has many wine to brown colored fibrils, especially near the center.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|last2=Sommer|first2=Robert|last3=Menge|first3=John A.|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|year=2012|isbn=978-0-520-95360-4|location=Berkeley|pages=229|oclc=797915861}}</ref> The [[lamella (mycology)|gills]] are close and white at first, turning pinkish and then dark brown in age.<ref name=":1">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=191–192}}</ref> The [[stipe (mycology)|stalk]] has a skirt-like [[annulus (mycology)|ring]] and is {{convert|4 to 20|cm|abbr=on|round=0.5|frac=8}} long, {{convert|1–3|cm|1|abbr=on|frac=8}} thick, white, and covered with soft woolly scales below the ring.<ref name=":0" /> The [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] is white and does not stain, and the odor and taste are mild.<ref name=":0" />

The purplish fibrous cap and shaggy white stem differentiate this mushroom from others which resemble it. Similar species include ''[[Agaricus hondensis]]'' and [[Agaricus moelleri|''A.&nbsp;moelleri'']].<ref name=":0" />

==Habitat and distribution==
The mushroom fruits in undisturbed mixed woods in Western North America and Japan.  It grows by itself or scattered in small clusters,<ref name=":1" /> often under [[redwood]], [[pine]], or [[alder]]. Recently this mushroom has been identified in New Zealand and Australia.<ref>[https://www.selbyshrooms.com.au/agaricus-subrutilescens-new-addition-australian-agaricus/ New edition. Australian Agaricus]selbyshrooms.com.au {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190519204817/https://www.selbyshrooms.com.au/agaricus-subrutilescens-new-addition-australian-agaricus/ |date=19 May 2019 }}</ref>
