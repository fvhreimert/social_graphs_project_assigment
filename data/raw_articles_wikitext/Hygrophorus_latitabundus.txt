'''''Hygrophorus latitabundus''''' is a species of [[fungus]] in the genus ''[[Hygrophorus]]''. It is distributed in European pine forests, and has a preference for calcareous soils. It fruits in autumn, producing large, edible mushrooms with slimy caps and stems.

==Description==
''Hygrophorus latitabundus'' fruiting bodies are large [[agaric]]s. The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is convex and slightly umbonate, coloured grey, brown and olivaceous with a darker, brownish centre. It is characteristically covered by a glutinous layer of slime, especially in wet weather conditions. The margin is inrolled. The cap diameter can reach 15&nbsp;cm, and as it matures, it flattens out. The white [[Lamella (mycology)|gills]] are thick, distant and have an adnate to weakly decurrent attachment to the stem.

The white [[Stipe (mycology)|stem]] is tall, fusiform, thick and robust. It is ornamented by numerous whitish flakes which are covered in a thick layer of slime. The flakes have a tendency to become brown. The flakes and slime extend from the base of the stem to the level of the margin, where they stop abruptly, creating a ring-like zone. Above this, the stem is white and visibly thinner. The stem is up to 15&nbsp;cm tall and 2–4&nbsp;cm thick.

The white [[Trama (mycology)|flesh]] is thick and firm, with a fungal smell and pleasant taste. An identification aid is the chemical reaction of the stem flesh when exposed to [[Ammonium hydroxide|ammonia solution]]. ==Habitat and distribution==

''Hygrophorus latitabundus'' is found in [[coniferous forest]]s, forming [[mycorrhiza|ectomycorrhizal]] relationships exclusively with [[pine]]s (''Pinus''). The fungus fruits in autumn and shows a preference for [[calcareous]] soils. Its occurrence is rare, though it is abundant there where it is established.<ref name=Elementichimici>{{cite report |author1=Cenci R. M. |author2=Cocchi L. |author3=Petrini O. |author4=Sena F. |author5=Siniscalco C. |author6=Vescovi L. | year=2010 | title=Elementi chimici nei funghi superiori |publisher=JRC Scientific and Technical Reports | language=Italian | page=175 | url=http://eusoils.jrc.ec.europa.eu/esdb_archive/eusoils_docs/other/EUR24415IT.pdf }}</ref>

Its distribution encompasses southern and central Europe, including Austria, Germany, France, Italy, Slovenia and Spain.<ref name=LeGrandLivre/><ref name=GBIFportal>{{cite web | url=http://data.gbif.org/species/14389569 | title=Species: ''Hygrophorus latitabundus'' Britzelm. 1899 | work=[[Global Biodiversity Information Facility]] | access-date=2010-12-25 }}</ref> It is also known to occur in the Republic of Macedonia, Greece and Turkey.<ref name=Macedonia>{{cite report | author=Petkovski S. | year=2009 | title=National Catalogue (Check List) of Species of the Republic of Macedonia | location=Skopje | url=http://www.protectedareas.mk/Matka/files/Final%20Report%20on%20National%20Catalogue.pdf | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100215141145/http://www.protectedareas.mk/Matka/files/Final%20Report%20on%20National%20Catalogue.pdf | archive-date=2010-02-15 }}</ref><ref name=Dimou>{{cite journal |author1=Dimou D. |author2=Zervakis G. |author3=Polemis E. | year=2008 | title=Mycodiversity studies in selected ecosystems of Greece: IV. ==Similar species==
Among the many [[List of Hygrophorus species|species of the genus ''Hygrophorus'']], there are some which can be mistaken for ''Hygrophorus latitabundus''. 
*The fruiting body of ''[[Hygrophorus persoonii|H. persoonii]]'' has a similar appearance, but the species is found only in deciduous forests with [[oak]] (''Quercus'') and [[beech]] (''Fagus''). Its flesh turns greenish with ammonia solution.
*''[[Hygrophorus olivaceoalbus|H. olivaceoalbus]]'' is a less robust species which often bears a dark, rough-banded stem ornamentation, and occurs with [[spruce]] (''Picea''), frequently in moss. Its stem flesh discolours to orange-red with ammonia solution.
