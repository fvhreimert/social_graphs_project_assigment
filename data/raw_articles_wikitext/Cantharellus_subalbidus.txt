'''''Cantharellus subalbidus''''', the '''white chanterelle''', is a species of [[fungus]] native to western [[North America]]. It is a member of the genus ''[[Cantharellus]]'' along with other popular edible [[chanterelle]]s.

==Description==
The mushroom is white to cream in color,<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Davis|first1=R. Michael|title=Field Guide to Mushrooms of Western North America|location=Berkeley|pages=272–273|oclc=797915861}}</ref> later darkening to yellow-orange.<ref name="amm09" /> The [[Pileus (mycology)|cap]] is {{convert|4-15|cm|round=0.5}} wide,<ref name="arora862" /> flat to depressed, becoming infundibuliform (vaselike) with age.<ref name=":0" /> The [[Stipe (mycology)|stalk]] is {{convert|2–7|cm|abbr=on|frac=4}} tall and 1–5&nbsp;cm wide, tapered, with yellow-brown spots due to bruising and age.<ref name=":0" /> The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are white, elliptical, and smooth.<ref name=":0" />

It is similar in appearance to other chanterelles except for its cream to white color and orange bruising.<ref name="plis04" />

===Similar species===
Several other species of chanterelle may be found in western North America:
*''[[Cantharellus californicus|C. californicus]]''
*''[[Cantharellus cascadensis|C. cascadensis]]''<ref name=Oregon/>
*''[[Cantharellus cibarius|C. cibarius]]'' var. ''roseocanus''
*''[[Cantharellus formosus|C. formosus]]''<ref name=Oregon/>

Additionally, ''[[Leucopaxillus albissimus]]'',<ref name=":0" /> ''[[Hygrophoropsis aurantiaca]]'', ''[[Chroogomphus|Chroogomphus tomentosus]]'', and species in the genera ''[[Clitocybe]]'',<ref name="arora862" /> ''[[Craterellus]]'', ''[[Gomphus (fungus)|Gomphus]]'', ''[[Omphalotus]]'', and ''[[Polyozellus]]'' may have a somewhat similar appearance.

==Distribution and habitat==
''C.&nbsp;subalbidus'' is native to [[California]] and the [[Pacific Northwest]] region of [[North America]]. In the latter, it can be found from August to October.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Seasonal Chart for Edible Mushrooms |url=https://www.centraloregonmushroomclub.com/Mushroom-Seasons.htm?m=102&s=866 |access-date=2024-03-31 |website=Central Oregon Mushroom Club}}</ref>

The fungus has been found to be more common in [[old-growth forest]]s than in younger forests.<ref name="dun06" />

==Ecology==
''Cantharellus subalbidus'' may form a [[mycorrhiza]]l association with species of [[pine]], [[Tsuga|hemlock]], [[Douglas-fir]], and [[Pacific madrone]].<ref name="amm09" /><ref name="mykoweb" /><ref name="arora86" />

==Uses==
A choice [[edible mushroom]], it can be prepared by being sautéed or cutting into chunks and baking at {{convert|350|F}} for 10 minutes.<ref name="Oregon">{{cite book |last1=Meuninck |first1=Jim |title=Foraging Mushrooms Oregon: Finding, Identifying, and Preparing Edible Wild Mushrooms |date=2017 |publisher=[[Falcon Guides]] |isbn=978-1-4930-2669-2 |page=5}}</ref>
