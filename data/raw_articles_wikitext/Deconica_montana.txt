'''''Deconica montana''''', commonly known as the '''moss-loving deconia'''<ref>{{Cite book |last=Siegel |first=Noah |title=Mushrooms of Cascadia: A Comprehensive Guide to Fungi of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Schwarz |first2=Christian |date=September 1, 2024 |publisher=Backcountry Press |isbn=9781941624197 |location=Humboldt County, CA |pages=105}}</ref> or '''mountain moss psilocybe''', is a common species of mushroom. Its appearance is that of a typical "[[Little Brown Mushroom#.22Little brown mushrooms.22|little brown mushroom]]" with a small, brown cap and a straight, thin stipe. It usually grows in [[moss]]y and [[montane]] regions around the world.

==Taxonomy==

''Psilocybe montana'' was formerly the [[type species]] of the mushroom genus ''[[Psilocybe]]''.<ref>Singer, R. (1975). ''The Agaricales in Modern Taxonomy''. J. Cramer, Vaduz. 912 p.</ref>  Because it does not contain [[hallucinogenic]] [[tryptamine]] derivatives like [[psilocybin]] or [[psilocin]],<ref name="Salazar1994">{{cite journal | last1 = Salazar | first1 = F. | last2 = Marcano | first2 = V. | last3 = Castellano | first3 = F. | last4 = Martinez | first4 = L. | last5 = Morales | first5 = A. | year = 1994 | title = Chemical and microstructural study of the genus ''Psilocybe'' (Agaricales) in the Venezuelan Andes: Part I. ''Psilocybe montana'' (Pers. ex Fr.) Kummer | journal = Ernstia | volume = 4 | issue = 1–2| pages = 11–19 }}</ref><ref name="pmid7967656">{{cite journal |author=Marcano, V. |author2=Morales Méndez, A. |author3=Castellano, F. |author4=Salazar, F. J. |author5=Martinez, L. |title=Occurrence of psilocybin and psilocin in ''Psilocybe pseudobullacea'' (Petch) Pegler from the Venezuelan Andes |journal=[[Journal of Ethnopharmacology]] |volume=43 |issue=2 |pages=157–9 |date=July 1994 |pmid=7967656 |doi= 10.1016/0378-8741(94)90013-2}}</ref> it does not stain blue when handled, unlike other typical hallucinogenic members of this genus. Molecular studies in the late 2000s revealed that the genus was [[polyphyletic]] and consisted of two distinct clades separating the blueing species from the non-blueing species.<ref name=Moncalvo2002/><ref name=Nugent2004/><ref name=Matheny2006/> Dividing the genus is problematic as the name ''Psilocybe'' was attached to ''P.&nbsp;montana'' and consequently to the non-blueing clade, leaving the hallucinogenic species without a generic name. Because the name is widely associated with the hallucinogenic species and considering the potential legal ramifications of changing their generic name, a proposal was made to conserve the name ''Psilocybe'' with [[Psilocybe semilanceata|''P.&nbsp;semilanceata'']] as the type. This left ''Deconica'' available as a name for the non-blueing species.<ref name=Redhead2005/> The proposal was unanimously accepted by the [[Nomenclature Committee for Fungi]] in 2009.<ref name=Norvell2009/>

== Description ==

The cap is 0.5–2&nbsp;cm in diameter, dome-shaped then convex or somewhat flat,<ref name="audu">{{Cite book |last=[[Audubon]] |title=Mushrooms of North America |publisher=[[Knopf]] |year=2023 |isbn=978-0-593-31998-7 |pages=666}}</ref> sometimes with a broad [[Umbo (mycology)|umbo]]. The cap is moist, [[glabrousness (botany)|glabrous]], [[hygrophanous]], and has radial striations to the center; the color is reddish-brown<ref name=":0">{{cite book|last1=Trudell|first1=Steve|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC|title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest|last2=Ammirati|first2=Joe|publisher=Timber Press|year=2009|isbn=978-0-88192-935-5|series=Timber Press Field Guides|location=Portland, OR|pages=208}}</ref> to dark-brown. The [[lamella (mycology)|gills]] are adnate to broadly adnate or sometimes very shortly [[decurrent gills|decurrent]], and of the same color as the cap. The [[Stipe (mycology)|stipe]] is 1.5–4&nbsp;cm long, 1–2&nbsp;mm thick, smooth, the same color as the cap, and brittle. The [[Basidiospore|spores]] are typically 7.5–10 × 6–8 × 5–5.5&nbsp;μm and [[:wikt:ovate|ovate]]–[[:wikt:lentiform|lentiform]] in shape with a thickened wall.<ref name="Miller1982">{{cite journal |jstor=3792745 | last1 = Miller | first1 = O. K. Jr. | last2 = Laursen | first2 = G. A. | last3 = Farr | first3 = D. F. | year = 1982 | title = Notes on Agaricales from Arctic Tundra in Alaska | journal = [[Mycologia]] | volume = 74 | issue = 4| pages = 576–591 |doi=10.2307/3792745 }}</ref> A large spored variety (spore dimensions of 8.5–11 × 6.0–8.5 × 5.0–7.0&nbsp;μm), ''Psilocybe montana'' var. ''macrospora'' Noordel. & Verduin (1999), has also been reported from the Netherlands.<ref name="isbn90-5410-492-9">{{cite book |author=Bas, Cornelis |title=Flora agaricina Neerlandica: critical monographs on families of agarics and boleti occurring in the Netherlands |publisher=A.A. Balkema |location=Rotterdam |year=1988 |isbn=978-90-5410-492-6 }}</ref> The [[spore print]] is dark greyish brown.<ref name="isbn3-7682-5474-7">{{cite book |author=Gastón Guzmán |title=The genus Psilocybe: a systematic revision of the known species including the history, distribution and chemistry of the hallucinogenic species |publisher=J. Cramer |location=Berlin |year=1983 |isbn=978-3-7682-5474-8}}</ref>

It is listed as inedible,<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |url=https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil |url-access=registration |year=2010 |publisher=Firefly Books |location=Buffalo, NY |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |page=[https://archive.org/details/mushroomsotherfu0000phil/page/230 230]}}</ref> being too small to be of interest.<ref name=":0" />

== Habitat and distribution ==

The species is commonly found in exposed situations such as dune-meadows, [[Heath (habitat)|heaths]] and tree-less [[tundra]], and open ''[[Pinus]]'' forests, usually on nutrient-poor, well-drained soil.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.entoloma.nl/html/psmontananed.html |title=Psilocybe montana page |website=www.entoloma.nl |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040530230811/http://www.entoloma.nl/html/psmontananed.html |archive-date=2004-05-30}} </ref>

==Ecology==
''Deconica montana'' is [[saprobic]], possibly also parasitic. It is often associated with mosses such as ''Brachythecium albicans'', ''B.&nbsp;mutabulum'', ''Campylopus introflexus'', ''[[Ceratodon purpureus]]'', ''[[Dicranum]] scoparium'', ''Eurhynchium hians'', ''E.&nbsp;praelongum'', ''E.&nbsp;speciosum'', ''Rhacomitrium canescens'', ''[[Pohlia]]'' species or ''[[Polytrichum]] piliferum''.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Lamoure |first1=D. |year=1977 |title=Agaricales de la zone alpine.
