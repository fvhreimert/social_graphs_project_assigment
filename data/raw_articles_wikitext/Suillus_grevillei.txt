'''''Suillus grevillei'''''<u>,</u> commonly known as '''tamarack jack''',<ref name="Arora1986">{{Cite book |last=Arora |first=David |author-link=David Arora |url=https://archive.org/details/arora-david-mushrooms-demystified-a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-fleshy-fungi-ten-speed-press-1986/page/497/mode/2up |title=Mushrooms Demystified: A Comprehensive Guide to the Fleshy Fungi |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |year=1986 |isbn=978-0-89815-170-1 |edition=2nd |location=Berkeley, CA |page=497 |orig-date=1979}}</ref> '''Greville's bolete''', or '''larch bolete''', is a [[mycorrhiza]]l mushroom with a tight, brilliantly coloured cap, shiny and wet looking with its mucous slime layer. The [[hymenium]] easily separates from the [[trama (mycology)|flesh]] of the cap, with a central [[stipe (mycology)|stalk]] that is quite slender. The species has a [[annulus (mycology)|ring]] or a tight-fitting annular zone.

==Etymology==
The [[specific epithet]] is derived from [[Robert Kaye Greville]].<ref>[http://natura.provincia.cuneo.it/funghi/scheda.jsp?id=418 natura.provincia.cuneo.it (In italian)], accessed 5 August 2008</ref>

==Description==
''Suillus grevillei'' is a mushroom with a 5&ndash;10&nbsp;cm (2&ndash;4 in) [[pileus (mycology)|cap]] colored from citrus yellow to burnt orange,<ref name="tfb">{{Cite book |last=Francis-Baker |first=Tiffany |title=Concise Foraging Guide |date=2021 |publisher=[[Bloomsbury Publishing|Bloomsbury]] |isbn=978-1-4729-8474-6 |series=[[The Wildlife Trusts]] |location=London |page=182}}</ref> that is at first hemispherical, then bell-shaped, and finally flattened. It has a sticky [[Plant cuticle|skin]], often with [[Veil (mycology)|veil]] remnants on the edge,<ref name=":0">{{cite book |last1=Trudell |first1=Steve |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WevHvt6Tr8kC&pg=PA223 |title=Mushrooms of the Pacific Northwest |last2=Ammirati |first2=Joe |publisher=Timber Press |year=2009 |isbn=978-0-88192-935-5 |series=Timber Press Field Guides |location=Portland, OR |page=223}}</ref> short tubes of yellow (possibly staining brownish)<ref name=":0" /> which descend down to the bottom of its cylindrical stalk (6&ndash;10 x 1–2&nbsp;cm), which is yellowish above the [[Annulus (mycology)|ring]] area with streaks of reddish brown below.<ref name=":0" /> The flesh is yellow, staining brown.<ref name=":0" />

The thin meat has consistency at first but then quickly becomes soft. It has an odor reminiscent of rumpled ''[[Pelargonium]]'' geranium leaves.{{citation needed|date=May 2025}}

==Habitat and distribution==
It grows only under [[larch]] trees.<ref name="Arora1986" /> Widespread in North America and Europe (July–November).<ref name="tfb" /> In Asia, it has been recorded from Taiwan.<ref name="Yeh 1980"/>

==Edibility==

''Suillus grevillei'' can be cooked as an [[edible mushroom]], though one without culinary  interest, if the slimy cuticle is removed from the cap.<ref>{{cite book |last=Phillips |first=Roger |title=Mushrooms and Other Fungi of North America |publisher=Firefly Books |year=2010 |isbn=978-1-55407-651-2 |location=Buffalo, NY |page=286}}</ref><ref name="tfb"/>

==Chemistry==
The fungus produces grevillin which is characteristic of this fungus. The genetic and enzymatic basis for [[atromentin]], the precursor to various pulvinic acid-type pigments, has been characterized (an atromentin synthetase by the name, GreA). A cosmid library (31 249 bp in total) has been made from the genome. The estimated gene density based on the cosmid library is 1 per 3900 bp of genomic DNA. The genome has a GC content of 49.8%.{{citation needed|date=May 2025}}
